# The Vanishing Tide — One-Shot Adventure

**System**: D&D 5th Edition
**Players**: 1 (with companion NPC)
**Level**: 3
**Duration**: 3-5 hours (one session)
**Tone**: Mystery/horror with moral complexity
**Setting**: Salthollow, a fog-shrouded coastal village on the Sword Coast

---

## Adventure Overview

The villagers of Salthollow are disappearing — taken from the shore during storms, one by one, over the past three months. Five are gone. No bodies. No traces. The player arrives on a stormy autumn night seeking shelter and is drawn into the mystery by a desperate constable and a grief-stricken old fisherman.

The investigation leads through the village, past an abandoned lighthouse, through a fisherman's graveyard, and into the sea caves beneath the headland. There, the truth waits: the "monster" is Maren, a sea hag — once Elara Tidecaller, the village's beloved healer, cursed by a dying warlock and cast out by the people she'd spent her life protecting. She's been taking villagers not to kill them, but to try to force their tears of remorse — the only thing that can break her curse. They sleep in magical stasis, alive and unharmed, while she grows more desperate and less human by the day.

The player must choose: destroy the hag, help break the curse, or walk away and leave Salthollow to its fate.

---

## The Hook

The player is traveling along the Sword Coast — the reason is their own (a job, a pilgrimage, a search for something). The storm catches them on an exposed stretch of coastal road with no shelter for miles in either direction. The lights of Salthollow appear through the fog.

> *"The storm hits like a fist. One moment you're walking into a stiff wind with your hood pulled low; the next, the sky opens and the rain comes sideways, cold enough to steal the breath from your chest. The road turns to mud beneath your boots. Lightning splits the dark, and in the flash you see it — a cluster of lights, dim and orange, huddled against the coastline below. A village. Shelter. The wind screams past your ears, and beneath it, if you strain, you can almost hear something else — thin and high and wrong, like singing from under the water. But that's the storm playing tricks. It must be. You put your head down and walk toward the lights."*

**DM Note**: The "singing" is real — it's Maren, calling from the caves. A DC 18 Perception check identifies it as a voice rather than wind. This plants the first seed but doesn't require follow-up yet.

## Act 1 — Arrival

### Scene 1: The Drowned Anchor Inn

The player arrives at The Drowned Anchor, cold and soaked. Thessa welcomes them with professional warmth — hot soup, a seat by the fire, an offer of a room. The common room is subdued. Fewer people than an inn this size should hold. The ones who are here drink quietly and glance at the shuttered windows.

> *"You push through the door and the warmth hits you like a blessing. The common room of The Drowned Anchor is low-ceilinged and fire-lit, smelling of wood smoke and fish stew. A woman behind the bar — tall, dark-haired, with a quick smile that doesn't reach her eyes — waves you toward the fire. 'Storm like this, you'd be mad to keep walking. Sit down. Soup's on the house.' A few faces turn your way from the scattered tables — fishermen, from the look of them, nursing their drinks in the heavy silence of people who've run out of comforting things to say to each other."*

**NPCs present**: Thessa (bar), Brine (end of bar, alone), Old Wen (fireside), 2-3 unnamed fishermen

### Information Available in Act 1

The player can learn the following through conversation. No checks needed for basic information — the village is desperate enough to talk freely.

**From Thessa** (freely offered):
- Five people have gone missing over the past three months
- It always happens during storms, always near the water
- The latest was Nessa, old Brine's daughter, three weeks ago
- The constable has imposed a curfew but it hasn't helped
- "We're a fishing village. We can't stop going to the water."

**From Old Wen** (will talk your ear off if you sit near him):
- This village has always had a difficult relationship with the sea
- He remembers a healer named Elara who lived here years ago — "Best woman this village ever had. Shame what happened."
- If asked about Elara: she tried to save a dying stranger and was "touched by his sickness." The village asked her to leave. He's vague on the details. DC 12 Insight: he's not lying, but he's leaving things out. He's ashamed.
- He knew the sea caves as a young man — "Dangerous, but there's dry chambers deep in, above the tide line"

**From Brine** (grudging, takes effort):
- His daughter Nessa was taken. He wants her back. He'll do whatever it takes.
- He's heard singing from the sea during storms. Not natural.
- He's seen lights in the abandoned lighthouse. Investigated once. Found claw marks. Left.
- He knows the sea caves and can guide someone there.
- DC 13 Persuasion or an offer of genuine help: "If you're going into those caves, I'm coming with you. Don't argue."

**From the notice board**:
- Missing posters for all five victims
- Constable Ward's curfew notice
- DC 12 Investigation on Ward's sketch map: the vanishing locations form a line pointing toward the lighthouse headland

### Scene 2: Constable Ward Arrives

After the player has been at the inn for about 30 minutes (or whenever the initial conversations are winding down), Ward arrives. He's soaked, tense, and visibly relieved to see someone who looks capable.

> *"The door bangs open and a man in a chain shirt stumbles in, streaming water. He scans the room, and his eyes lock on you — specifically on whatever weapon you're carrying. He straightens up, touches the tarnished badge on his chest, and walks over. 'Constable Haddon Ward. You're not from here, which right now makes you the most useful person in this village. Can I buy you a drink and ruin your evening with a story about people who've stopped existing?'"*

Ward lays out the situation formally: five missing, no leads, no help coming from the regional lord. He offers 100 gold from the village fund for anyone who can stop the vanishings. He's honest about what he doesn't know, which is most of it.

**DM Note**: This is where the player formally accepts the quest. If they need convincing, a fresh scream of wind outside and the muffled sound of distant singing (DC 15 Perception to hear through the walls) can provide urgency.

### Transition to Act 2

The player has the basic picture: people vanishing, storms, the sea, something wrong. Now they need to investigate. The logical threads are:

1. The lighthouse (lights seen there, Brine's report of claw marks)
2. The graveyard (Old Wen mentioned Elara; Father Callum tends the graveyard)
3. Ward's investigation files (at the guardpost)
4. Pressing Thessa for more information (she's holding back)

The player can pursue these in any order. Brine will offer to accompany them.

## Act 2 — Investigation

### Investigation Threads

The player should discover the following before entering the caves. They don't need ALL of it — any two or three threads will give them enough to proceed. But the more they learn, the better prepared they'll be for the moral choice in Act 4.

#### Thread A: The Lighthouse

See `world/locations.md` for full details. Key discoveries:
- The makeshift camp — someone inhuman has been staying here
- The tally marks — fifteen years of daily counting
- The claw marks — humanoid but wrong
- The ruined journal — fragments of Elara's story, including her plan to take villagers
- The hidden trapdoor — passage to the sea caves

**Encounter**: No combat at the lighthouse, but atmosphere is thick. If the player arrives during a lull in the storm, they can hear singing from below the trapdoor — DC 12 Perception. Brine is visibly shaken by the claw marks and the journal.

**Brine's reaction to the journal**: He doesn't recognize the handwriting. He says, quietly, *"Whoever wrote this... they were a person once. A person who was hurting."* If the player connects the dots to Elara, Brine goes quiet for a long time and then says, *"I didn't stop them. The night they drove her out. I stood in my doorway and I didn't stop them."*

#### Thread B: The Graveyard

See `world/locations.md` for full details. Key discoveries:
- Elara's grave — cracked headstone, recent offerings, the inscription "May the Sea Forgive Us"
- Voss's empty grave — the curse consumed his remains
- The blackened earth — traces of the original curse

**Encounter**: At the graveyard, the player may encounter 2 **Skeletons** (CR 1/4, 50 XP each) that rise from disturbed graves near Voss's plot. The necrotic taint from the curse animates them.

**Skeleton** (x2):
- AC 13, HP 13, Speed 30 ft.
- Shortsword: +4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing
- Shortbow: +4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing
- Vulnerable to bludgeoning
- Immune to poison, exhaustion
- CR 1/4 (50 XP each)

*DM Note: These skeletons are a symptom, not a clue. They're here because the curse's residue is strong enough to disturb the dead. The real information is on the headstones.*

**Father Callum** (if the player visits the chapel or finds him at the graveyard): Confirms Elara was buried here. Says the grave was dug but no body was ever placed — it was a memorial, commissioned by "someone in the village" (Thessa, if pressed, DC 13 Persuasion). He's noticed the offerings at the grave. He doesn't know who leaves them. He's frightened and will bless the player's weapons if asked (+1d4 radiant damage on next hit, single use — a minor boon for his help).

#### Thread C: Ward's Files

At the guardpost, Ward's investigation notes provide a systematic picture. The player can review them with Ward or alone (Ward trusts them with the key). Key takeaways:
- The vanishing locations form a directional pattern pointing toward the lighthouse headland
- All vanishings occurred during storms with onshore winds
- Tide was always rising when the vanishings happened
- The singing that witnesses report is described consistently: "like a lullaby, but sad" and "coming from under the water"
- Ward notes: *"If I didn't know better, I'd say something is calling them to the shore."*

#### Thread D: Pressing Thessa

The hardest thread but the most rewarding. Thessa won't give up her secret easily.

**Stage 1 — Deflection**: Thessa brushes off questions about Elara. "Old story. Ancient history. Drink up." DC 15 Insight: she's lying. Not about the facts, but about how much it matters to her.

**Stage 2 — Cracks**: If the player mentions specific evidence (the journal, the grave, the inscription), Thessa flinches. She pours herself a drink. "Where did you hear that name?" DC 13 Persuasion or presenting physical evidence: she admits she knew Elara. "We were friends. Once."

**Stage 3 — The Truth**: Getting the full confession requires one of:
- Attitude reaching Allied (difficult in one night without the locket)
- Finding the lockbox and the unsent letter independently (DC 15 Investigation in her room)
- Presenting the ruined journal from the lighthouse: Thessa recognizes the handwriting. She breaks.
- DC 18 Persuasion with genuine empathy (not intimidation): "I think whoever is taking people was someone who used to be one of you. And I think you know who."

**If Thessa confesses** (see `world/npcs/thessa.md` for her full confession speech), the player learns:
- Elara was cursed by the dying warlock Voss. The curse was transforming her.
- The village, led by Elder Sera, drove Elara out. Thessa's testimony sealed the vote.
- Thessa has the matching pendant — she'll give it to the player.
- Thessa believes the vanishings are Elara's revenge and that she deserves to be taken.

**The Pendant**: If Thessa gives the player the sea-stone pendant, it becomes a key item. It grants safe passage through the Warded Passage in the caves, and if shown to Maren, it cracks her hostile exterior immediately.

### Encounter: The Shore at Midnight (Optional)

If the player goes to the shoreline during the storm (perhaps following the singing), they can witness a vanishing attempt in progress. Maren, in her illusory disguise, is wading out of the surf. If the player is near the water, she sees them and retreats — but not before the player gets a glimpse.

> *"A figure stands at the waterline, waist-deep in the churning surf, facing the village. It's the shape of a woman — hunched, old, draped in rags. She doesn't seem to feel the cold. As lightning flashes, you see her turn toward you. For an instant, just an instant, the lightning strips the illusion away, and you see — something else. Something grey and wrong and weeping. Then the dark closes and she is gone, sinking into the water as smoothly as a seal."*

DC 16 Investigation (if the player examines the spot): clawed footprints in the wet sand, already filling with water. They lead from the sea and go back to the sea.

This encounter is optional but powerful — it confirms that the threat comes from the water and gives the player a visceral encounter with the mystery before entering the caves.

## Act 3 — The Depths

### Entering the Caves

The player can enter via the lighthouse trapdoor (if they found it) or by climbing down the cliff face at low tide (DC 15 Athletics; DC 10 with a rope). Brine accompanies them and provides guidance on the cave layout and tidal dangers.

**Brine's guidance**:
> *"Tide'll be low for another two hours. That's our window. The lower passages flood when the tide comes in — water rises fast, no warning. The upper chambers stay dry. Whatever's in here, it'll be deep. Past where I went as a boy. Past where anyone sensible goes."*

**Environmental hazard — the tide**: If the player takes too long in the lower caves (more than 2 hours of in-game time), the tide begins rising. Water floods the lower passages at a rate of 1 foot per 10 minutes. The entry passage becomes impassable within 30 minutes of the tide turning. This creates urgency but does not trap the player — the upper passages and sanctum are above the waterline.

### Cave Encounters and Exploration

See `world/locations.md` for detailed descriptions of each area. The progression is:

1. **Entry Passage** — Atmospheric descent. No encounters.
2. **Tidal Gallery** — The main cave. Giant crabs (optional combat), ancient paintings, branching passages. The player must choose a path.
3. **Right passage (dead end)** — Giant crab lair. Loot: 15 gp, potion of healing. A red herring that rewards exploration.
4. **Left passage to Warded Passage** — The barrier that guards the inner caves. A puzzle/skill challenge.
5. **Stasis Chamber** — Discovery of the living villagers. Major revelation. Brine's emotional breakdown.
6. **The Sanctum** — Confrontation with Maren.

### Encounter: Giant Crabs (Tidal Gallery)

2 **Giant Crabs** attack if the player disturbs them or takes the right passage.
- See stat blocks in `world/locations.md`
- Brine fights one, player fights one
- Quick fight, 1-2 rounds. XP: 50 total.

### Challenge: The Warded Passage

This is a skill challenge, not a combat encounter. See `world/locations.md` for full details on ways past.

**DM Guidance**: The ward should feel dangerous but not impassable. Give the player multiple options. If they have the pendant from Thessa, they walk through freely. If they speak Elara's name, the ward shudders and lets them pass. If they try to analyze it magically, reward the Arcana check. Force is an option but costs HP.

**Brine's reaction**: He touches the ward and jerks his hand back, hissing. He can't pass by force — his HP is too valuable. But if the player finds a peaceful way through, Brine follows, grumbling about magic.

### The Stasis Chamber — The Revelation

This is the emotional peak of Act 3. The player finds the five missing villagers alive, suspended in magical stasis.

**Key moments**:
- The player sees all five villagers alive and realizes the "monster" hasn't been killing anyone
- Brine sees Nessa and breaks down. Play this with weight: *"The old man drops his harpoon. It clatters on stone and he doesn't notice. He crosses the chamber in three strides and presses both hands against the shimmering water that holds his daughter. His mouth works but no sound comes. Then, very quietly, he says her name. And again. And again. And his shoulders begin to shake."*
- DC 13 Arcana: the stasis is gentle, the villagers are unharmed, they can be released
- DC 15 Arcana: the dreams the villagers are having are designed to provoke guilt — they're being shown what the village did to Elara
- The player can release the villagers now (see locations.md for mechanics) or proceed to the sanctum first

**DM Note**: If the player tries to release the villagers, Maren senses this and calls out from deeper in the caves: *"Don't. Please. If you wake them, I lose my only chance. Come speak with me first. Please."* The voice is ragged, desperate, and recognizably human despite its inhuman timbre. The player chooses whether to listen.

## Act 4 — Confrontation

### Meeting Maren

The player enters the Hag's Sanctum to find Maren waiting on her rock shelf above the scrying pool. How the encounter unfolds depends on the player's approach and what they've learned.

#### If the player arrives hostile (weapons drawn, no attempt at parley):

> *"She drops from the rock shelf with a hiss, landing in a crouch. The illusion falls away — or maybe she lets it fall — and you see her true form. Grey-green skin stretched over too-long bones. Hair like black kelp. Claws like fishhooks. And those eyes — pale, weeping, terribly human in that inhuman face. 'Another hero,' she rasps. 'Come to slay the monster. Come on, then. COME ON.'"*

Combat begins. See Maren's stat block and combat tactics in `world/npcs/maren.md`. Brine fights alongside the player. This is a CR 2 encounter with a companion — challenging but winnable for a level 3 character.

**Combat notes**:
- Maren's Horrific Appearance triggers immediately. Both player and Brine must save (DC 11 WIS). Brine can use his Superstitious Ward for one of them.
- Maren fights defensively, using terrain. She does NOT want to kill. She'll call out Elara's lines even during combat.
- If reduced to half HP (26), she pleads: *"Please — I don't want this. I never wanted this."*
- If reduced to 15 HP, she offers to release the villagers.
- If killed: her form flickers back to Elara for one instant. The pendant crumbles. The stasis ends. The villagers wake, confused and cold.

#### If the player arrives cautiously (weapons sheathed, attempting conversation):

> *"A voice comes from the shadows above the pool. It's rough, broken, like someone who hasn't spoken in years trying to remember how. 'You came through the ward without force. That means you're either very clever or very foolish.' A pause. 'Or you have her pendant.' The figure shifts on the rock shelf, and you see the outline of something humanoid but wrong. The weeping eyes catch the blue light. 'Why are you here? The village sent you to kill me, yes? Then kill me. Or sit down and hear a story. Your choice.'"*

Maren will talk. She's been alone for fifteen years and the chance to tell her story to someone who might listen is almost overwhelming. She shifts between the bitter rasp of the hag and the educated cadence of the healer, sometimes mid-sentence.

**Information Maren shares freely**:
- Her name was Elara. She was the village healer.
- She tried to save a dying stranger. His curse passed to her.
- The village drove her out. Her best friend testified against her.
- The curse has transformed her. She fights it every day. She's losing.
- She took the villagers to try to make them cry — genuinely cry, with real remorse. The curse demands tears from those who wronged her.
- She hasn't hurt any of them. They sleep. They dream.
- She's running out of time. Weeks, maybe days, before Elara is gone completely.

**Information Maren shares reluctantly (DC 13 Persuasion or presenting the pendant/journal)**:
- She needs the genuine tears of at least three people who wronged her
- The stasis isn't working — the sleepers don't dream deeply enough to truly weep
- She knows who organized the mob (Elder Sera) and who sealed her fate (Thessa)
- She doesn't want to hurt anyone. The urge to is the hag's nature, and she fights it constantly.
- She's terrified of what she's becoming

#### If the player has the pendant:

Showing Maren the matching sea-stone pendant is the most powerful opening. She goes silent. Her claws touch the pendant at her own throat. When she speaks, it's Elara, not Maren:

> *"...Thessa kept it. After everything... she kept it."*

This shifts her attitude from Hostile to Indifferent immediately. She'll talk freely and is far more receptive to negotiation.

### The Moral Choice

After the player understands the full situation, they must decide what to do. Present the options clearly but don't force a choice — let the player think, ask questions, and plan.

#### Option 1: Break the Curse (The Redemption Path)

**Requirements**: Gather three genuine sets of tears from those who wronged Elara.

**How**:
1. **Brine** — Already on the verge if he's seen Nessa. The player can remind him of his guilt: he watched from his doorway and said nothing. A DC 12 Persuasion check or genuine roleplay is enough. Brine weeps for Elara and for his own cowardice.
2. **Thessa** — If she's been brought to the caves (or if the player returns to fetch her), confronting her with Maren's true identity and the pendant breaks her open. She weeps without needing a check — this has been building for fifteen years.
3. **Elder Sera** — Must be awakened from stasis. She's the hardest. She's proud and frightened and doesn't want to admit what she did. DC 15 Persuasion, or DC 12 if Thessa and Brine have already wept and Sera can see the effect. Or the player can use their own words, their own appeal.

**The Ritual**: When three sets of genuine tears fall in Maren's presence, the curse reacts. Read-aloud:

> *"The tears hit the stone floor, and the cave fills with light — not the blue-green of the algae, but warm gold, like sunrise. Maren screams. It's a terrible sound, but it's not pain — or not only pain. It's the sound of something breaking open. Her body convulses, the grey-green skin cracking like old plaster, and beneath it — beneath it, you can see skin. Human skin. She reaches toward the light with hands that are shrinking, the claws receding, the fingers becoming fingers again. And then she falls. And the woman on the cave floor, shaking and weeping and small and terribly human, is Elara Tidecaller. She is thin and scarred and her hair is still dark as kelp. But her eyes — her eyes are brown, and warm, and fully, completely her own."*

The villagers in stasis wake. The curse is broken. Elara will need time and care to recover, but she is herself again.

#### Option 2: Kill the Hag (The Pragmatic Path)

The player fights and kills Maren. See combat notes above.

**Aftermath**:
- The stasis ends. Villagers wake, confused and cold but unharmed.
- Maren's body reverts to Elara for one instant before dissolving into sea foam.
- The pendant crumbles.
- The caves begin to collapse slowly — the player has time to get everyone out but shouldn't linger.
- The village is grateful. Ward pays the 100 gp reward. But Brine is quiet, and Thessa won't meet anyone's eyes.
- If the player learned the full truth, this ending has weight. They didn't kill a monster. They killed a victim.

#### Option 3: Broker a Deal (The Compromise Path)

The player negotiates a ceasefire. Maren releases the villagers. In exchange, the village must:
- Acknowledge what they did to Elara
- Provide Maren with supplies and solitude — she keeps the caves
- Allow her to visit Elara's grave
- Work toward finding a permanent cure

**DC 15 Persuasion** to get Maren to agree (DC 12 with the pendant). **DC 15 Persuasion** to get Ward/the village to agree (DC 12 if Thessa advocates for it).

**Aftermath**:
- The villagers are released and go home
- Maren retreats to the caves. The curse remains but progresses slowly
- An uneasy peace. The village knows its shame. Maren knows she's not forgotten.
- The story isn't over — but the immediate crisis is resolved.

#### Option 4: The Dark Bargain (The Temptation)

If the player seems inclined, Maren offers an alternative: help her take the remaining people she needs (specific villagers who were part of the mob). In exchange, she'll share a secret of the sea — a magic item or boon.

This is the "evil" option. The player becomes complicit in kidnapping.

**If accepted**: Maren provides a **Cloak of the Manta Ray** (wondrous item, requires attunement — grants swim speed 60 ft, breathe underwater). She asks the player to lure 2 more specific villagers to the shore.

**DM Note**: Don't judge the player for considering this. Present it as a real option with real consequences. But make it clear — through Brine's horror, through the player's own conscience — what they'd be doing.

## Possible Endings

### Ending 1: Redemption

The curse is broken. Elara is restored. The village must reckon with what they did, but there's hope — a healer returned, a community confronting its worst moment. Brine is reunited with Nessa. Thessa has her friend back.

**Rewards**: 100 gp from Ward, Thessa gives the player the sea-stone pendant as a token (worth 50 gp, or useful as a focus/trinket), 450 XP from the sea hag encounter (resolved nonviolently), 100 XP each for the skeletons (if fought), 25 XP each for giant crabs, plus 200 XP quest completion bonus.

**Epilogue**:
> *"Dawn comes to Salthollow. The storm has broken, and the first light turns the fog to gold. You stand in the village square as the freed captives reunite with their families. Brine holds Nessa so tight you worry he'll crack her ribs. Thessa and Elara sit on the inn's front step, not touching, not speaking, just — being near each other. Constable Ward presses a pouch of gold into your hand and says, 'I don't know what you did down there. But thank you.' The sea is calm. For the first time in months, the sea is calm."*

### Ending 2: The Hag Slain

Maren is dead. The villagers are safe. But the truth — that the village created its own monster — lingers like smoke. Those who knew Elara carry a heavier burden now. The graveyard gets a new offering: sea lavender on an empty grave.

**Rewards**: 100 gp from Ward, 450 XP for defeating the sea hag, 100 XP for skeletons, 50 XP for giant crabs, 200 XP quest completion bonus. Loot from the sanctum: 35 gp in assorted coins, a *driftwood wand* (functions as an arcane focus), a *potion of water breathing*.

**Epilogue**:
> *"The village celebrates, after a fashion. Drinks are poured and toasts are raised. But Brine sits alone, turning a crumbled pendant over and over in his hands. Thessa locks up early and doesn't come down for breakfast. At the graveyard, someone has placed fresh sea lavender on Elara's headstone. The crack in the stone seems deeper than before. You leave Salthollow with gold in your pocket and a question in your chest that has no comfortable answer: when a village makes a monster, who is the villain?"*

### Ending 3: Uneasy Peace

A truce holds. Maren lives. The villagers are free. Nobody is satisfied, but everybody is alive. The story continues beyond the player's departure.

**Rewards**: 100 gp from Ward, 200 XP for quest completion (no combat XP for the hag), 100 XP for skeletons, 50 XP for giant crabs, 100 XP negotiation bonus.

**Epilogue**:
> *"You leave Salthollow on a clear morning, the road drying in pale autumn sun. Behind you, the village goes about its business — mending nets, hauling pots, living. In the sea caves below the headland, something that used to be a woman tends a garden of sea lavender and watches the village through a pool of still water. The peace is fragile. A thread of guilt and grief and stubborn hope, stretched between a village that wronged and a woman who endured. It might hold. It might not. But today, the boats go out, and the sea is calm, and nobody is missing."*

### Ending 4: The Dark Path

The player accepted the bargain. They carry a gift from the hag and a stain on their soul. Brine, if he learned of the betrayal, becomes an enemy. The village's nightmare continues. And somewhere in the caves, a hag smiles with a human woman's mouth.

**Rewards**: Cloak of the Manta Ray, whatever gold the player already earned or took. No quest completion XP. The player's alignment shifts one step toward evil (or gains a flaw: "I made a deal with a monster and I'd do it again").

**Epilogue**:
> *"You walk out of Salthollow before dawn, the cloak heavy on your shoulders, the taste of brine in your mouth. Behind you, the village sleeps. It won't sleep soundly for long. You tell yourself it was the practical choice. You tell yourself they deserved it — some of them, anyway. You tell yourself a lot of things as the road unwinds beneath your feet and the sound of the sea fades behind you. It never quite fades completely."*

## Scaling Notes for Solo Play

### Companion NPC: Brine

Brine is essential for keeping a solo level 3 character alive in combat. Guidelines:

- **In combat**: Brine acts on his own initiative, controlled by the DM. He focuses on the nearest enemy and fights defensively (uses Dodge if not engaged). He follows the player's tactical suggestions.
- **Out of combat**: Brine provides information about locations and the sea but doesn't solve puzzles or make decisions. He offers opinions when asked.
- **Spotlight**: Brine should never overshadow the player. He's support. If the player is doing well, Brine hangs back. If the player is struggling, Brine steps up.
- **Death**: Brine CAN die, but he shouldn't die randomly. If he falls in combat, it should be dramatic and meaningful. Give the player a chance to stabilize him.

### Encounter Balance

| Encounter | Enemies | Difficulty (Solo L3 + Brine) | Notes |
|-----------|---------|------------------------------|-------|
| Graveyard Skeletons | 2 Skeletons (CR 1/4) | Easy | Warm-up, atmospheric |
| Giant Crabs | 2 Giant Crabs (CR 1/8) | Easy | Quick, cave flavor |
| Maren (combat) | 1 Sea Hag (CR 2) | Medium-Hard | Main event. Horrific Appearance is the real danger. Death Glare only works on frightened targets — this is the hag's one-two punch. If the player saves against Horrific Appearance, the fight is manageable. If they fail, Death Glare becomes lethal. Brine's Superstitious Ward exists for this reason. |

### If the Player Struggles

- Brine can absorb hits with his 40 HP
- Father Callum's blessing (+1d4 radiant on one hit) gives a damage boost
- The potion of healing in the right cave passage (if found) provides emergency recovery
- In the Maren fight, she pulls punches narratively — she doesn't want to kill

### If the Player Breezes Through

- Add a third skeleton at the graveyard (emerges from Voss's empty grave)
- The giant crabs in the gallery join the fight (4 total instead of 2)
- Maren fights more aggressively — uses terrain, hit-and-run tactics, tries to separate the player from Brine

### Atmosphere Reminders

- The storm never fully stops until dawn. Use it: rattling shutters, howling wind, distant thunder.
- The singing from the sea recurs. It gets clearer as the player gets closer to the truth.
- Light sources matter in the caves. Describe what the torchlight reveals and what it doesn't.
- Cold is constant. The player is wet, chilled, and far from comfort. Make them feel it.
- Silence is as powerful as sound. The moments after Brine sees Nessa. The pause before Maren speaks. The quiet after the curse breaks.
- This is horror, but it's sad horror. The monster weeps. The villain is the village. The scariest thing in the cave is a fifteen-year-old betrayal.
