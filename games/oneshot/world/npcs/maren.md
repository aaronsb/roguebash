# Maren, the Sea Hag — Formerly Elara Tidecaller

## Overview

**True Name**: Elara Tidecaller
**Current Name**: Maren (she barely remembers her old name)
**Race**: Fey (formerly human)
**Age**: 37 (cursed at 22, has aged beyond her years)
**Location**: The Hag's Sanctum, deepest chamber of the sea caves
**Role**: Antagonist with tragic dimension. The moral crux of the adventure.

## The Story of Elara

Elara Tidecaller was the best thing that ever happened to Salthollow. She arrived as a young woman with a gift for herbalism and folk magic — not a cleric or wizard, but something gentler. A hedge healer. She cured fevers, set bones, eased births, and brewed remedies from kelp and sea lavender. The village loved her. She loved them back.

Fifteen years ago, a stranger washed ashore during a storm: a warlock named Voss, half-dead and raving. His patron — some deep thing from the Far Realm — had turned on him, and a killing curse was eating him from the inside. Elara took him in. She tried to save him. She tried everything.

Voss died on the third night. As his last breath left him, the curse — a living thing, hungry for a host — leapt from his body into hers. The only person touching him. The only person who cared enough to be at his bedside.

The transformation was slow. Over weeks, Elara's skin grew pale, then grey. Her fingers lengthened. Her hair fell out in clumps and grew back lank and dark as kelp. She tried to hide it. She kept healing. But the villagers noticed. They grew afraid.

Elder Sera called a village meeting. Thessa — Elara's closest friend — was asked to speak. Thessa was terrified. She told the village what she'd seen: Elara talking to the sea at night, her reflection in the water not matching her form, crabs gathering at her doorstep in unnatural numbers. The village voted. Elara had to go.

They came for her with torches. Not to kill her — they weren't murderers. Just to drive her out. Elara begged. She said she could fight the curse. She said she just needed time. They gave her none. Brine watched from his doorway and said nothing. Thessa wouldn't meet her eyes.

Elara fled to the sea caves. Over the years, the curse completed its work. The woman called Elara died by inches, and the thing called Maren was born.

## Maren's Plan

Maren discovered — through instinct, through the curse's own dark knowledge — that the transformation could be reversed. She needed the tears of genuine remorse from those who wronged her. Not forced tears. Not crocodile tears. Real grief, real guilt, real sorrow for what they did.

She tried calling to the villagers from the caves. They blocked their ears. She tried sending dreams. They warded their houses with iron and salt. Finally, in desperation, she began taking them — pulling them into the sea during storms, carrying them to her sanctum, placing them in magical stasis. She crafted enchanted dreams for each one: visions of Elara's last night, the torches, the begging, the betrayal. She hoped the dreams would crack their hearts open.

It hasn't worked. The stasis is too deep. They sleep but do not truly dream. They weep but the tears are not real — just reflexes of sleeping bodies. Maren is running out of time. The curse grows stronger. Soon there will be nothing of Elara left at all.

## Appearance

### True Form (Sea Hag)

> *"She unfolds from the shadows like something the tide washed in and forgot. Her skin is the grey-green of drowned flesh, stretched tight over too-long bones. Lank hair like black kelp hangs past her waist, dripping with brine. Her fingers end in dark claws, curved like fishhooks. Her eyes — her eyes are the worst part. They are pale blue, almost white, and they are weeping. Even through the horror of that face, even through the wrongness of every angle and proportion, those eyes are weeping. And they are human."*

### Illusory Appearance

When using Illusory Appearance, she looks like an old, weathered fisherwoman — ugly, hunched, but not inhuman. This is how she approaches the shore during storms. Anyone who gets close enough to touch her can feel the wrongness (DC 16 Investigation to see through without touching).

### Remnants of Elara

Around her neck, barely visible beneath rags, she wears a silver pendant set with a blue-green sea stone — a gift from Thessa, from when they were friends. She hasn't been able to bring herself to remove it. This is a key plot item: Thessa has the matching pendant, hidden in a lockbox under the inn's floorboards.

## Personality

- **Bitter**: She was cast out by people she loved. That wound has festered for fifteen years.
- **Desperate**: The curse is winning. She can feel herself slipping further away from Elara with each passing month.
- **Not Evil**: Despite her hag form, she has not killed. She feeds on fish and seaweed. She placed the villagers in gentle stasis — they are not suffering. She is horrified by what she's becoming but cannot stop it alone.
- **Lonely**: She has spoken to no one in fifteen years. The sound of a voice that isn't screaming at her may be enough to give her pause.
- **Conflicted**: Part of her — the Maren part, the hag part — wants to hurt them. Wants them to suffer as she suffered. She fights this urge constantly. It's getting harder.

## Speech Patterns

Shifts between the educated, gentle cadence of the healer Elara and the hissing, bitter rasp of the hag Maren. Sometimes mid-sentence. The more emotional she gets, the more Elara surfaces.

**As Maren (hostile, guarded)**:
> *"Another one come to kill the monster? Get in line. The crabs will pick your bones cleaner than theirs."*

**As Elara bleeding through (vulnerable)**:
> *"I healed their children. I sat with their dying. And they gave me — they gave me torches and a stone road to walk in the dark..."*

**Losing control**:
> *"NO. No, you don't get to say her name. Elara is dead. They killed her. THEY KILLED HER. I'm what's left. I'm what they MADE."*

**If shown genuine kindness**:
> *"...you're not from the village, are you. I can tell. You don't smell like their fear. Why are you here? Why do you... why do you care?"*

## What Maren Wants

1. **Break the curse** (primary). She needs genuine tears of remorse — at least three people who wronged her must truly weep for what they did.
2. **Be seen as Elara again** (hidden desire). She wants someone — anyone — to look at her and see the woman she was, not the monster she's become.
3. **Punish the village** (the hag's desire, which she resists). This urge grows stronger every day.

## What Maren Knows

- The curse came from Voss's patron, a Far Realm entity. She doesn't know which one.
- The curse can be broken by genuine tears of remorse from those who wronged her. She needs at least three.
- The villagers in stasis are unharmed and can be released at any time. She holds them as leverage and as subjects for her dream experiments.
- Elder Sera (among the taken) was the one who organized the mob. Thessa was the one whose testimony sealed the vote. Brine was the one who watched and did nothing.
- The curse is accelerating. She estimates she has weeks, maybe days, before Elara is gone completely and only the hag remains.
- She knows the sea caves completely — every passage, every tidal chamber, every danger.

## Stat Block — Maren (Sea Hag)

*Medium fey, chaotic neutral (not chaotic evil — she is fighting the curse's influence)*

- **AC** 14 (natural armor)
- **HP** 52 (7d8+21)
- **Speed** 30 ft., swim 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 16 (+3) | 13 (+1) | 16 (+3) | 12 (+1) | 12 (+1) | 13 (+1) |

- **Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 11
- **Languages** Aquan, Common, Giant
- **CR** 2 (450 XP)

**Amphibious.** Maren can breathe air and water.

**Horrific Appearance.** Any humanoid that starts its turn within 30 feet of the hag and can see the hag's true form must make a DC 11 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the creature is frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, with disadvantage if the hag is within line of sight, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to the hag's Horrific Appearance for the next 24 hours. Unless the target is surprised or the revelation of the hag's true form is sudden, the target can avert its eyes and avoid making the initial saving throw. Until the start of its next turn, a creature that averts its eyes has disadvantage on attack rolls against the hag.

### Actions

**Claws.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 10 (2d6+3) slashing damage.

**Death Glare.** The hag targets one frightened creature she can see within 30 feet of her. If the target can see the hag, it must succeed on a DC 11 Wisdom saving throw against this magic or drop to 0 hit points.

**Illusory Appearance.** The hag covers herself and anything she is wearing or carrying with a magical illusion that makes her look like an ugly creature of her general size and humanoid shape. The effect ends if the hag takes a bonus action to end it or if she dies. The changes wrought by this effect fail to hold up to physical inspection. A creature must take an action to visually inspect the illusion and succeed on a DC 16 Intelligence (Investigation) check to discern that the hag is disguised.

### Special: Stasis Tide (Unique Ability)

**Stasis Tide (Recharge 5-6).** Maren channels the curse's power to place a creature in magical stasis. One creature within 10 feet must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or fall into a magical sleep (as per the spell, but not limited by HP). The creature remains in stasis until Maren releases them or until the spell is dispelled (treat as a 3rd-level spell). Maren can maintain up to 6 creatures in stasis simultaneously.

*DM Note: Maren will not use Stasis Tide in combat unless she's trying to capture rather than kill. If the player engages her in genuine conversation, she won't use it at all.*

## Combat Tactics (If Fight Occurs)

- Maren does NOT want to fight. She will warn, threaten, and posture first.
- If combat begins, she opens with Horrific Appearance (automatic when her true form is visible).
- She targets the player with Claws, saving Death Glare for if they become frightened.
- She uses the cave terrain: knocking lanterns into water (darkness), retreating through flooded passages (swim speed advantage), using narrow passages to prevent flanking.
- If reduced to half HP, she calls out as Elara: *"Please — please, I don't want to hurt anyone. I never wanted to hurt anyone."* This is genuine, not a trick.
- If reduced to 15 HP or below, she will offer to release the villagers in exchange for her life.
- She will not fight to the death willingly. If escape is possible, she flees deeper into the caves.

## The Cure

The curse can be broken through one of these methods:

1. **Tears of Remorse** (the "correct" solution): Three or more people who participated in casting Elara out must weep genuine tears of remorse in her presence. Thessa, Brine, and Elder Sera (who can be awakened from stasis) are the most likely candidates. This requires roleplay — the player must facilitate this.

2. **Ritual of Unbinding** (the "arcane" solution): A DC 18 Arcana check (or a character with Remove Curse cast at 5th level or higher) can identify that the curse could be temporarily suppressed if Maren is immersed in holy water and the matching pendant (Thessa's locket) is pressed to the sea-stone pendant around her neck while someone speaks Elara's full name three times. This doesn't permanently break the curse but gives Elara control for one month, during which more permanent solutions can be sought.

3. **Death** (the "simple" solution): Killing Maren ends the curse but also kills Elara. The villagers in stasis wake. The pendant around her neck crumbles to sand as she dies, and for one instant, her form flickers back to the young healer she once was.
