# Thessa — Innkeeper of The Drowned Anchor

## Overview

**Full Name**: Thessa Greymantle
**Race**: Human
**Age**: 39
**Occupation**: Owner and operator of The Drowned Anchor Inn
**Location**: The Drowned Anchor Inn, Salthollow village center
**Role**: Key informant, guilt-ridden witness, moral fulcrum of Act 2

## Appearance

> *"The woman behind the bar moves with the practiced efficiency of someone who hasn't stopped working in years. She's handsome rather than pretty — strong jaw, dark hair pinned back with two bone combs, sleeves rolled to the elbows over forearms corded with muscle from hauling kegs. Her smile comes easy when you walk in, warm and automatic as a hearth fire. But her eyes don't match it. Her eyes are tired, and watchful, and carry something heavier than the tray of drinks she sets down."*

Thessa is taller than most women in the village, with an upright bearing that suggests someone who refuses to be diminished. She wears practical clothes — a wool dress under a heavy apron — and a thin leather cord around her neck that disappears beneath her collar. (This holds the matching pendant to Maren's sea-stone necklace, though she keeps it hidden.)

## Personality

- **Hospitable**: Running an inn is all she knows. She's good at it. Warm fires, strong drinks, a ready smile. She makes people feel welcome because that's what the job requires.
- **Guarded**: Beneath the warmth is a wall. She deflects personal questions with jokes or more drinks. She changes the subject when the conversation turns to the past.
- **Guilty**: She testified against her best friend and has carried that weight for fifteen years. She drinks alone after closing. She has nightmares. She leaves offerings at Elara's grave.
- **Afraid**: The vanishings terrify her because she suspects — though she won't admit it — that she knows who's behind them. And she knows she deserves to be taken.
- **Brave, when it counts**: If pushed to the breaking point, Thessa's courage surfaces. She's not a coward. She was one, once, and she's spent fifteen years hating herself for it.

## Speech Patterns

Warm and brisk with customers. Clipped and defensive when pressed. When she finally opens up, her voice drops and the warmth becomes real instead of professional.

**As innkeeper (professional warmth)**:
> *"Storm like this, you'd be mad to keep walking. Sit down, I'll get you something hot. Room's two silver if you want a bed. Soup's free — can't charge a soul for soup on a night like this."*

**Deflecting questions about the past**:
> *"Elara? That's an old name. Old story. Drink up — you don't want to hear an old woman's tales when there's ale to be had."*

**When cornered/confronted**:
> *"You don't know what it was like. You didn't see her changing. You didn't watch your best friend becoming... something else. We were afraid. I was afraid. Is that what you want to hear? That I was a coward? Fine. I was a coward. And now she's — now they're all —"*

**If she confesses fully**:
> *"I was supposed to speak for her. At the meeting. She asked me to. She held my hands and said 'Tell them I'm still me, Thessa. They'll believe you.' And I walked into that meeting and I told them everything I'd seen. Every strange thing. Every sign that she was changing. I told them because I was afraid of what she was becoming, and I wanted them to take her away so I wouldn't have to watch. I killed her. Not with a blade. With words. And I've been dying of it ever since."*

## What Thessa Knows

- She knows the "monster" is almost certainly Elara. She hasn't admitted this to anyone.
- She knows the full story of Elara's curse — she was there when Voss arrived, watched Elara tend him, saw the change begin.
- She was Elara's closest friend. They were like sisters. Thessa's betrayal at the village meeting was the act that sealed Elara's fate.
- She has a matching pendant to Elara's — a pair of sea-stone necklaces they bought together from a traveling merchant. She keeps it in a locked iron box beneath the floorboards of her private room.
- She leaves salt and dried flowers at Elara's grave in the cemetery. She's been doing this for fifteen years.
- She knows that Elder Sera organized the mob, that Constable Ward's father (the previous constable) enforced it, and that most of the village went along with it.
- She does NOT know that the taken villagers are alive. She assumes they're dead and blames herself.

## What Thessa Wants

1. Absolution. She wants to be forgiven, but she doesn't believe she deserves it.
2. The vanishings to stop — not because she fears for herself, but because more people are suffering for what she did.
3. If she learns the villagers are alive and there's a way to save them AND Elara, she will do anything to help. Anything.

## Thessa's Secret: The Locket

Hidden beneath the floorboards in Thessa's private room (behind the bar, up a narrow staircase) is a small iron lockbox. Inside:
- The matching sea-stone pendant
- A pressed sprig of sea lavender (Elara used to wear it in her hair)
- A folded letter, never sent, addressed to Elara. It reads: *"I'm sorry. I'm sorry. I'm sorry. I should have spoken for you. I should have stood between you and the torches. I should have been your friend when you needed one. I wasn't. I am the worst thing I know. If you can hear me from wherever the sea has taken you, know that I have not slept a full night since. I will never sleep a full night again. You deserved better than me. You deserved better than all of us. — T."*

**Finding the lockbox**: DC 15 Investigation check in Thessa's room, or Thessa reveals it herself if her attitude reaches Allied.

## DM Notes

- Thessa is the emotional key to the adventure. If the player can earn her trust and get her to confess, it opens the path to the peaceful resolution.
- Don't make her confession easy. She's been keeping this secret for fifteen years. It takes real effort — empathy, evidence, persistence, or the right pressure point (the locket, mentioning Elara by name, showing her the ruined journal from the lighthouse).
- If the player is aggressive or threatening, Thessa shuts down completely. She'll serve drinks and say nothing. Intimidation works short-term (DC 15 to get fragments) but permanently damages the relationship and locks out the confession path.
- If the player finds the locket independently and presents it to Thessa, she breaks down immediately. This is the fastest path to her full cooperation.
- Thessa will go to the caves if asked, but she's terrified. She'll need encouragement. If she goes and faces Maren, her tears are genuine — this counts toward breaking the curse.
