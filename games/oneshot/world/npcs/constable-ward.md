# Constable Ward — Village Law of Salthollow

## Overview

**Full Name**: Haddon Ward
**Race**: Human
**Age**: 34
**Occupation**: Constable of Salthollow (appointed, not elected)
**Location**: Village square guardpost / The Drowned Anchor Inn
**Role**: Quest giver, information source, voice of pragmatic authority

## Appearance

> *"The man who approaches you wears a chain shirt under a wool cloak that's soaked through and steaming faintly in the firelight. A short sword hangs at his hip, and a badge of tarnished copper is pinned to his chest — the Salthollow anchor-and-wave. He's younger than you expected. Tired, too. The kind of tired that sleep doesn't fix. He looks at you the way a drowning man looks at a passing ship: hope he's trying very hard not to feel."*

Ward is lean, clean-shaven, with close-cropped brown hair and grey eyes that sit in dark hollows. He carries himself with the rigid posture of a man trying to project authority he's not sure he has. His hands are steady, but his jaw clenches when he talks about the vanishings.

## Personality

- **Pragmatic**: Ward deals in what's in front of him. Evidence, footprints, timelines. He doesn't have time for ghost stories — except the ghost stories are starting to be the only explanation left.
- **Overwhelmed**: He's a village constable, not an adventurer. He handles bar fights and stolen fishing nets. Five disappearances in three months is beyond anything he was prepared for.
- **Honest**: He won't lie to the player. If he doesn't know something, he says so. If something frightens him, he admits it. This honesty is his best quality.
- **Desperate**: He's sent letters to the regional lord requesting help. No response. The player is the first capable-looking person to arrive in weeks.
- **Young for his post**: His father was the previous constable — the one who enforced Elara's banishment. His father died two years ago. Ward doesn't know the full story of what happened fifteen years ago. He was a child when it occurred and was told Elara "went mad and had to leave."

## Speech Patterns

Clear, direct, slightly formal. Uses law-enforcement language. Gets more informal and raw as the night wears on.

**Official mode (when first meeting)**:
> *"I'm Constable Ward. I keep the peace in Salthollow — or I did, before the peace started keeping itself by removing people from it. Five gone in three months. No bodies. No tracks after the tide line. I'll be straight with you: I need help. Name your price."*

**Sharing information**:
> *"Pattern's clear enough. Storms. Night. Near the water. Always someone alone. First was old Pell, the night watchman. Then Corwin the cooper. Then Mab. Then Elder Sera. Then Nessa — that's Brine's girl. Every two to three weeks. If the pattern holds, we're due."*

**Admitting fear**:
> *"I've walked the shoreline every storm night since it started. Sword drawn, lantern lit. You know what I found? Nothing. Not a footprint, not a scrap of cloth, not a drop of blood. Things that take people leave traces. This doesn't. That means it's not a 'thing' in any sense I'm equipped to handle."*

## What Ward Knows

- Full timeline of the vanishings: dates, names, locations, weather conditions
- Each victim was alone and near the shoreline during a storm
- No bodies, no blood, no signs of struggle
- Several villagers report hearing singing from the sea during storms
- The lighthouse has shown strange lights, but his two deputies refuse to investigate it (they quit last month)
- He's heard the old stories about Elara but was told she "went mad." He doesn't know the full truth about the curse or the mob.
- He searched the shoreline caves once at low tide but didn't go deep — the passages are dangerous and he was alone.
- He suspects something supernatural but has no framework for dealing with it.

## What Ward Does NOT Know

- That Maren is Elara
- That the villagers are alive in stasis
- The full story of what the village did to Elara
- Thessa's involvement in the banishment

## What Ward Wants

1. The vanishings stopped, by whatever means necessary.
2. To protect the remaining villagers.
3. Justice — but he's pragmatic enough to accept "everyone survives" as a form of justice.

## DM Notes

- Ward is the player's natural first contact and quest giver. He provides the initial framework for the investigation.
- He will NOT accompany the player into the caves. He'll offer, but he should be convinced to stay and protect the village. If the player insists, he comes but is clearly out of his depth.
- Ward is useful for validating player theories. If the player figures something out, Ward can confirm it from his investigation notes.
- If the player returns from the caves with the truth, Ward's reaction depends on the evidence: he'll be shocked, angry at the village elders, but ultimately focused on the practical question of "what do we do now?"
- In the peaceful resolution, Ward can help organize the village to face Maren/Elara and can be the voice of "we need to make this right."
- Ward should feel like a good man in an impossible situation. He is not the enemy. He is not part of the old guilt. He's just trying to do his job.
