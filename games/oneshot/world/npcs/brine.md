# Brine — Old Fisherman of Salthollow

## Overview

**Full Name**: Brine (born Brennan Ashwick; hasn't gone by that in decades)
**Race**: Human
**Age**: 62
**Occupation**: Retired fisherman, now mends nets and repairs boats
**Location**: The Drowned Anchor Inn (his home is near the docks, but he's been sleeping at the inn since Nessa was taken)
**Role**: Companion NPC — fights alongside the player in the sea caves

## Appearance

Brine is built like a barrel lashed to two fence posts — short, broad, weathered to the color of old rope. His hands are scarred and calloused, his fingers permanently curved from decades of hauling nets. A white beard, tangled and unkempt, hangs to his chest. His left eye is milky — blinded in a storm twenty years ago — but his right eye is sharp as a fishhook. He wears a salt-stained wool coat over leather armor he dug out of his sea chest. A harpoon with a blackened iron head leans against whatever wall is nearest.

> *"You see him before you hear him — a squat, weatherbeaten man hunched over a tankard at the bar's end. His one good eye tracks you the moment you walk in, measuring you the way a fisherman measures a wave: how much trouble are you, and can I ride you out?"*

## Personality

- **Gruff**: Speaks in short, clipped sentences. Doesn't waste words. Doesn't suffer fools.
- **Superstitious**: Touches iron when he hears bad news. Won't say certain words at sea. Believes in signs and omens. Spits over his left shoulder.
- **Loyal**: Once he decides you're worth a damn, he'll follow you into the dark. No complaints. No hesitation. He's buried friends before. He'll do it again if he has to.
- **Grieving**: His daughter Nessa is among the taken. He won't say it directly, but everything he does is about getting her back. The fear that she's dead is eating him alive, but he won't admit it.
- **Knows the sea**: He knows the tides, the caves, the currents, the weather. The sea is the one thing he trusts completely, even though it's taken everything from him.

## Speech Patterns

Short sentences. Nautical metaphors. Drops pronouns. Doesn't ask questions — makes statements that expect answers.

**Examples**:
> *"Storm'll break by midnight. Maybe. Sea's got a mind of her own."*

> *"Don't like the caves. Don't like what's in 'em. But my girl's down there, so that's where I'm going."*

> *"Knew Elara, back when. Good woman. Don't know what she is now."*

> *"Iron and salt. That's what keeps the dark things honest. Iron and salt."*

## What Brine Knows

- The vanishings started three months ago. Always during storms. Always near the water.
- His daughter Nessa was taken three weeks ago. He searched the shore for days. Found nothing.
- He's heard singing from the sea during storms — thin, high, wrong somehow.
- He knows the sea caves beneath the lighthouse. Fished near them as a young man. They flood at high tide but there are dry chambers deeper in.
- He remembers Elara the healer from fifteen years ago. She was kind. She healed his wife's fever once. He wasn't part of the mob that drove her out, but he didn't stop them either. This shames him.
- He's seen lights in the old lighthouse. Went to investigate alone once. Found claw marks and fled.
- He does NOT know that Maren is Elara. He suspects something supernatural but thinks it's a sea spirit or demon.

## What Brine Wants

1. Find Nessa. Everything else is secondary.
2. Kill whatever took her — unless the player can convince him otherwise.
3. Protect the village, in his gruff and reluctant way.

**Multiattack.** Brine makes two harpoon attacks.

**Harpoon (Spear).** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 5 (1d6+2) piercing damage, or 6 (1d8+2) piercing damage if used with two hands.

**Harpoon (Thrown).** *Ranged Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, range 20/60 ft., one target. *Hit:* 5 (1d6+2) piercing damage. Brine carries 2 harpoons.

**Handaxe.** *Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. *Hit:* 5 (1d6+2) slashing damage. Backup weapon.

## Combat Tactics (DM Notes)

- Brine fights defensively. He uses his shield and stays near the player.
- He'll throw a harpoon to open an encounter, then close to melee.
- If Nessa is in danger, all tactics go out the window — he'll charge straight for her.
- Against the sea hag, he's frightened (Horrific Appearance) but will keep fighting. His Superstitious Ward can help with this once.
- He won't attack Maren if the player has convinced him she's Elara and there's a chance to save her. But he'll be conflicted and need reassurance.
- If reduced to 10 HP or below, he doesn't retreat — he fights harder. He's not leaving without Nessa.

## Companion NPC Guidelines

- Brine follows the player's lead in most situations. He'll offer gruff advice but won't override player decisions.
- In combat, the DM controls Brine's actions, but the player can suggest tactics.
- Brine should never steal the spotlight. He's support, not the protagonist.
- He provides useful information about locations and the sea, but doesn't solve puzzles for the player.
- His emotional arc (fear for Nessa, guilt about Elara, confronting the hag) should complement, not overshadow, the player's story.

## Emotional Arc

1. **At the inn**: Gruff, suspicious, measuring the player
2. **Agreeing to help**: Reluctant hope. Trying not to show it.
3. **In the caves**: Focused, competent, but increasingly tense
4. **Finding the stasis chamber**: Overwhelmed. Seeing Nessa alive breaks something open in him. He may weep — the first tears he's shed in years.
5. **Confronting Maren**: If he learns she's Elara, he's shattered by guilt. He'll beg the player to find another way than violence.
6. **Resolution**: Depending on the ending, either grateful beyond words, or carrying a new grief.
