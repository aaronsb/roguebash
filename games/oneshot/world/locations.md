# Locations — Salthollow

## Overview of Salthollow

> *"The village clings to the coast like a barnacle to a hull — stubborn, weatherbeaten, refusing to let go. Two dozen buildings of grey stone and salt-bleached timber huddle around a muddy square, their windows shuttered tight against the gale. Beyond them, a crescent harbor shelters a handful of fishing boats that rock and strain at their moorings. The air tastes of brine and chimney smoke. A single road — more rut than road — climbs inland through wind-stripped scrub and vanishes into the fog. To the north, a headland rises where the black finger of an old lighthouse points at the sky. To the south, a bluff of pale stone overlooks the sea, crowned with leaning grave markers. And below everything, at the base of the cliffs, the ocean pounds and roars and waits."*

**Population**: ~120 (before the vanishings), now ~115
**Economy**: Fishing, crabbing, some salt harvesting
**Governance**: Village council (currently reduced — Elder Sera is among the taken), with Constable Ward keeping order

## The Drowned Anchor Inn

**Type**: Tavern and inn
**Owner**: Thessa Greymantle
**Location**: East side of the village square

### Exterior

> *"The inn is the largest building in Salthollow, which isn't saying much. Two stories of stone on the ground floor and timber above, with a sign that swings and screams on its hinges — a painted anchor wrapped in kelp, so faded by salt air that it looks like it's drowning. Warm light leaks through the shutters. The door is heavy oak, iron-banded, and when you push it open, the wind tries to follow you in."*

### Common Room (Ground Floor)

> *"Heat hits you first — a wall of it, after the cold. A stone fireplace dominates the far wall, big enough to roast a pig, and the fire in it is doing its best to fight back the damp. The room smells of wood smoke, fish stew, wet wool, and something yeasty — bread or beer, hard to tell. Half a dozen tables of scarred oak fill the space, most empty. A bar of dark wood runs along the left wall, bottles and casks ranked behind it. The floorboards creak with every step. This place has been soaking up salt air and spilled ale for a hundred years, and it shows."*

**Key features**:
- Fireplace with mantel (carved with anchors and fish — a tradition in coastal taverns)
- Bar with 6 stools
- 6 tables, seating about 24 total
- A notice board near the door (see below)
- Stairs at the back leading up to rooms
- A door behind the bar leading to the kitchen and Thessa's private quarters

**NPCs typically present**:
- Thessa (behind the bar)
- Old Wen (by the fire, always)
- Brine (end of the bar, nursing a drink)
- 2-3 other villagers on any given night (fishermen, frightened, drinking more than usual)
- Constable Ward (comes and goes)

### Notice Board

Pinned to a corkboard near the front door:

- **"MISSING"** posters for Pell, Corwin, Mab, Elder Sera, and Nessa. Hand-drawn likenesses, increasingly desperate wording. Nessa's is the most recent — the ink hasn't fully dried.
- A **faded notice** from the regional lord about road maintenance (months old, ignored).
- An **official-looking proclamation** from Constable Ward imposing a curfew: no one is to walk the shoreline alone after dark.
- A **handwritten prayer** pinned with a rusty nail, asking Lathander for protection. The handwriting is shaky.
- A **crude map** of the village and surrounding area, drawn by Ward, marking the locations where each person vanished. **DC 12 Investigation**: the marks form a rough line pointing northwest toward the lighthouse headland.

### Guest Rooms (Upper Floor)

Four small rooms for travelers. Simple: a bed, a washbasin, a candle, a window with shutters. The player is given Room 3 — it overlooks the harbor.

### Thessa's Private Room (Behind the Bar, Upstairs)

Small, tidy, sparse. A bed, a writing desk, a wardrobe. Under the floorboards near the bed: the iron lockbox with the matching pendant and Thessa's unsent letter. **DC 15 Investigation** to find without being told.

## The Village Square and Notice Board

**Type**: Open area, village center
**Location**: Center of Salthollow

> *"It's generous to call it a square. It's a patch of packed mud and gravel where four paths meet, anchored by a stone well with a wooden cover chained shut — nobody uses the well at night anymore. A few market stalls stand empty and dripping, their canvas awnings snapping in the wind. The chapel of Lathander sits on the west side, a small stone building with a single stained-glass window that glows faintly amber. The constable's guardpost — barely more than a shed with a desk — squats beside it."*

**Key features**:
- The well (chained shut after dark — superstition says things climb up from underground)
- Empty market stalls (usually active during the day)
- The chapel of Lathander (Father Callum tends it)
- The guardpost (Ward's base of operations, contains his investigation notes)
- Iron lamp posts (three, lit at dusk by whoever Ward can convince to go outside)

**Ward's Investigation Notes** (in the guardpost):
A careful, methodical file. Dates, times, weather conditions, interview summaries. DC 10 to find in the unlocked guardpost. Contains:
- Dates and locations of all five vanishings
- Weather reports (all storms, all at night)
- Interview notes from witnesses (mostly "heard singing" or "saw nothing")
- A sketch map matching the notice board but with more detail — tide times, moon phases
- A note in the margin: *"Lighthouse? Need to check. Can't go alone."*

## The Old Lighthouse

**Type**: Abandoned structure
**Location**: Northern headland, 20-minute walk from the village along a cliff path

> *"The path narrows as the headland rises, winding between tufts of sea grass and exposed rock slick with rain. The lighthouse appears through the fog like a dead tree — a round stone tower, forty feet high, its whitewash long since scoured away by wind and salt. The iron railing around the top has rusted to lace. The door hangs on one hinge. No light burns in the lamp room, but something about the darkened windows at the top gives the impression of eyes."*

### Exterior

- The cliff path is slippery in the rain: DC 10 Acrobatics or Athletics to navigate safely at speed. Failure means the character slips and takes 1d4 bludgeoning damage from the rocks, plus falls prone.
- Around the base: tide pools, exposed rock, guano-streaked boulders. A narrow set of carved steps leads down the cliff face toward the sea caves (easy to miss: DC 14 Perception, or DC 10 if the player is specifically looking for a path down).

### Ground Floor

> *"The door groans open to reveal a circular room stripped of anything useful. Sand and dead kelp have blown in through the broken windows. The iron staircase spiraling up the interior wall is rusted but holds when you test it. Against the far wall: a bedroll, stained dark with sea water. A ring of stones holds cold ashes. Fish bones. And on the wall above the makeshift camp, scratched deep into the stone with something sharp — tally marks. Rows and rows of them. You count quickly. Over five thousand."*

**Key discoveries**:
- **The camp**: Someone has been sleeping here, off and on. The bedroll is damp but used recently. DC 12 Survival: the occupant is not human — the way the bedroll is arranged suggests someone with a different body shape. Longer limbs.
- **The tally marks**: 5,475 marks, roughly. Fifteen years of daily tallies. DC 10 Intelligence: that's approximately fifteen years. Whoever is counting is counting the days since something happened.
- **Claw marks**: On the door frame, the walls, the stair railing. DC 12 Nature: these aren't animal claws. Too regular. Five fingers, but the nails are long and curved.
- **The journal** (DC 13 Investigation to find — wedged in a crack in the wall):

> *The journal is small, leather-bound, swollen with water damage. Most pages are illegible — blurred ink and mold. But fragments survive:*
>
> *"...the change is worse today. My hands. I can't hold a quill properly anymore. The nails won't stop growing..."*
>
> *"...tried to go back. Stood at the edge of the village in the dark. Could see the lights in the Anchor. Could see HER through the window. Couldn't make myself step forward. What would I say? What would she see?..."*
>
> *"...the sea talks to me now. Not words. Feelings. Hunger. Loneliness. I think it's the curse. I think the curse is the sea and the sea is the curse and I am drowning on dry land..."*
>
> *"...I know how to break it. The tears. Their tears. They have to MEAN it. They have to feel what they did. But how? How do I make them feel it when they won't even say my name?..."*
>
> *"...took the first one tonight. The night watchman. He screamed. I didn't want him to scream. I just need him to cry. To really cry. To be sorry. Is that so much? Is that so much to ask?..."*

**DC 12 Arcana**: The journal radiates faint traces of transmutation magic — consistent with a polymorph-type curse.

### Lamp Room (Top Floor)

The old oil lamp is broken. But there's a small, battered tin lantern up here that Maren uses to see the village from above. A DC 10 Perception check reveals scratches on the window facing the village — claw marks, as if someone has spent many hours looking down at Salthollow.

### Hidden Trapdoor (Ground Floor)

Behind the bedroll, beneath a flat stone that doesn't quite match the others: a trapdoor. DC 13 Investigation, or DC 10 if the player moves the bedroll deliberately. It opens onto carved stone steps leading steeply down into darkness — the passage to the sea caves.

## The Fisherman's Graveyard

**Type**: Cemetery
**Location**: Southern bluff overlooking the sea, 10-minute walk from the village

> *"The graveyard sits on a bluff where the wind never stops. Headstones lean at angles like drunken sailors, many so weathered that the names have been sanded smooth. The newer stones are towards the back, but 'newer' here means decades rather than centuries. Wrought-iron fences, gone red with rust, mark family plots. Sea grass grows between the graves, tall and whispering. The sound of the waves below is constant — a low, rhythmic thunder that makes the ground hum. At the far edge, where the bluff drops sheer to the water, a single grave stands apart from the rest."*

### Key Features

**Elara's Grave**:
> *"The headstone is cracked — not from age, but from a deliberate blow. Someone took a hammer to it, years ago. The pieces have been fitted back together with care, though the crack still shows. The inscription reads: 'Elara Tidecaller — Healer of Salthollow — May the Sea Forgive Us.' At the base of the stone, partially sheltered from the rain by the overhang: a small pile of offerings. Dried sea lavender, bundled with twine. A scattering of salt crystals. A single silver coin, tarnished green. Someone tends this grave. Someone who feels they owe a debt."*

- **DC 12 Investigation**: The offerings are recent — within the last few days. The sea lavender matches a variety that grows near the inn.
- **DC 13 Insight**: The inscription is strange for a banished person. "May the Sea Forgive Us" suggests collective guilt, not condemnation. Whoever commissioned this stone was asking forgiveness.
- **DC 15 History or asking Old Wen**: The stone was commissioned by Thessa Greymantle, five years after the banishment. She paid for it herself.

**The Open Grave**:
> *"Two rows back from Elara's stone, a grave has been torn open. Not dug up — clawed out from below. The earth around the hole is blackened and stinking, as if something poisonous seeped up from the soil. The headstone reads 'Voss' — no surname, no dates, no epitaph. The grave is empty."*

- **DC 13 Nature or Arcana**: The blackened earth is tainted by necrotic energy. Whatever was in this grave didn't stay dead — or something reached in and pulled the remnants out.
- **DC 15 Arcana**: The curse that killed Voss left a residue. When it transferred to Elara, it hollowed out his remains — consumed them for power. The empty grave means the curse was thorough. There's nothing left of Voss but the stain.

**Other Graves**: Generations of Salthollow's dead. Fishermen, mostly. Many graves marked "Lost at Sea" with no body beneath. A disproportionate number of young deaths. This village has always paid a toll to the ocean.

## The Sea Caves

**Type**: Natural cave system, partially flooded
**Location**: Beneath the lighthouse headland, accessible via the lighthouse trapdoor or by a treacherous climb down the cliff face (DC 15 Athletics)
**Tidal Note**: The lower passages flood at high tide. The upper passages and the sanctum remain dry. The caves are most accessible in the two hours before and after low tide.

### Entry Passage

> *"The steps descend into salt-smelling darkness. The stone is wet, the walls close — barely wide enough for one person. You can hear the sea ahead, not crashing but breathing, a deep rhythmic pulse that vibrates in your chest. After forty feet, the passage opens into a wider space, and you feel the air change — colder, damper, alive with the sound of dripping water."*

- The passage from the lighthouse trapdoor descends about 40 feet via carved stone steps.
- **Dim light** from bioluminescent algae on the walls (blue-green, faint, beautiful despite the circumstances).
- The alternative entrance from the cliff face is a 15-foot wide opening at sea level, reachable only at low tide.

### Tidal Gallery

> *"The cave opens into a long gallery, perhaps sixty feet long and twenty wide, with a ceiling lost in shadows. The floor is a mix of sand, rock, and tidal pools that glow faintly blue. Crabs scuttle away from your light — dozens of them, more than you've ever seen in one place. Passages branch off to the left and right. The sound of the sea comes from everywhere. Along the right wall, faded paintings — ancient, predating the village — show spiraling patterns and shapes that might be waves or might be tentacles."*

**Key features**:
- Tidal pools (some contain useful things: a silver ring dropped by one of the taken villagers, DC 12 Perception)
- The crab swarm: Dozens of crabs. Not hostile, but unsettling. They watch. DC 14 Nature: their behavior is unnatural — coordinated, as if guided by an intelligence.
- Ancient cave paintings: DC 15 History or Arcana: these are old. Very old. Pre-dating the village by centuries. They depict some kind of sea entity — possibly the same Far Realm patron that cursed Voss.

**Branching passages**:
- Left: leads to the Warded Passage and beyond to the Stasis Chamber and Sanctum
- Right: leads to a dead end with a tidal pool (floods at high tide). A **giant crab** (CR 1/8) lairs here and will fight if cornered. This is a red herring but contains a waterlogged satchel with 15 gp and a potion of healing.
- Straight ahead: slopes down to the sea entrance (floods first when the tide rises)

### Encounter: Giant Crabs

If the player explores the right passage or disturbs the crabs in the gallery, 2 **Giant Crabs** attack.

**Giant Crab** (x2)
- AC 15, HP 13, Speed 30 ft./swim 30 ft.
- Claw: +3 to hit, 1d6+1 bludgeoning, target grappled (escape DC 11)
- CR 1/8 (25 XP each)

*DM Note: This is a warm-up fight. Brine handles one while the player fights the other. Quick and atmospheric — describe the echoes, the splashing, the eerie blue light.*

### The Warded Passage

> *"The passage narrows again, and you feel it before you see it — a pressure in the air, like walking into a headwind that isn't there. The walls are scratched with symbols, deep and deliberate, glowing faintly green. And in the middle of the passage, stretching from floor to ceiling like a curtain of dark water standing on edge — a barrier. It shimmers. It hums. When you reach toward it, your hand goes numb."*

**The Ward**: Maren placed this magical barrier to protect her sanctum. It deals 2d6 cold damage to anyone who tries to pass through it by force (DC 13 CON save for half).

**Ways past**:
- **Arcana DC 15**: Identify the ward's nature — it's keyed to intent. Anyone who approaches without hostile intent can pass (the ward reads surface emotions). The player must genuinely set aside aggression to pass peacefully. This requires a Persuasion or Insight check (DC 13) to center oneself, or roleplay of putting away weapons and speaking peaceful words.
- **Dispel Magic or similar**: Suppresses the ward for 1 minute.
- **Force**: Take 2d6 cold damage, CON save DC 13 for half. The ward reasserts after 1 round but is weakened — subsequent forced passages deal only 1d6.
- **Thessa's pendant**: Holding the matching sea-stone pendant grants free passage. The ward recognizes it.
- **Speak Elara's name aloud**: The ward shudders and thins for 1 round, allowing passage without damage. Maren hears this and is alerted.

### The Stasis Chamber

> *"Beyond the ward, the passage opens into a cathedral of stone. The ceiling vaults upward, lost in darkness. And there, arranged in a circle on raised stone platforms — five figures, motionless, cocooned in shimmering curtains of seawater that hang in the air like frozen waterfalls. You can see their faces through the water. Their eyes are closed. Their chests rise and fall, impossibly slow. They are breathing. They are alive.*
>
> *A young woman with dark hair — Nessa, it must be. A broad man in a cooper's apron. A thin woman with weaver's calluses. A young fisherman, barely twenty. And an elderly woman with a stern face even in sleep — Elder Sera, the one who organized the mob."*

**Key features**:
- The five taken villagers in magical stasis — alive, unharmed, sleeping
- Each cocoon of suspended seawater is maintained by small carved symbols on the stone platforms
- **DC 13 Arcana**: The stasis is gentle. These people are not suffering. They're dreaming — though the dreams seem troubled (furrowed brows, twitching fingers, the occasional murmured word)
- **DC 15 Arcana**: The stasis can be ended by Maren's will or by disrupting the carved symbols. Disrupting the symbols violently risks injuring the sleepers (1d6 cold damage each). Carefully scratching out the symbols (DC 12 with a tool, takes 1 minute per person) releases them safely.
- If Brine sees Nessa, he drops his harpoon and goes to her. He presses his hand against the water cocoon. His voice breaks. He says her name. *This may be the first genuine tears the adventure produces — and they count toward breaking the curse if Maren is present to witness them.*

### The Passage Beyond

A narrow, natural tunnel leads from the Stasis Chamber deeper into the cliff. The bioluminescence intensifies. The sound of the sea fades. The air grows warmer, humid, smelling of salt and something floral — sea lavender. This leads to the Hag's Sanctum.

## The Hag's Sanctum

**Type**: Natural cave, modified
**Location**: Deepest chamber of the sea cave system

> *"The tunnel opens into a space that shouldn't exist — a grotto, round and smooth, as if carved by a patient hand over centuries. The ceiling is covered in bioluminescent algae so thick and bright it looks like a night sky — blue-green stars on a dark stone heaven. A pool of perfectly still water fills the center of the chamber, clear to its sandy bottom. Around the pool, clusters of sea lavender grow from cracks in the rock, their purple flowers impossibly vivid in the blue light. The walls are covered in carvings — not the ancient spirals from the gallery, but newer ones. Pictures. A village. A woman with long hair, smiling, holding flowers. The same woman, twisted, alone, weeping. The same woman, clawed hands raised to a face that is no longer hers.*
>
> *And in the far corner, hunched on a shelf of rock above the pool, watching you with pale eyes that weep without stopping — she is here. She has been waiting."*

### Features

- **The Scrying Pool**: The still pool in the center shows images of the surface when Maren wills it. This is how she watches the village and chooses her targets. DC 14 Arcana to identify as a scrying device.
- **The Wall Carvings**: Maren's autobiography in stone. Years of lonely work. They tell her story more clearly than words: the healing, the curse, the betrayal, the transformation, the loneliness. Any player who takes time to study them understands the full story without needing to be told. DC 10 Investigation to piece together the narrative.
- **Sea Lavender Garden**: Maren tends these flowers. They're the same variety Elara used to wear in her hair. A tiny act of remembering who she was.
- **The Pendant**: Visible around Maren's neck if the player looks (DC 10 Perception). Matches Thessa's pendant exactly.

### The Confrontation

This is where the adventure reaches its climax. See `scenario.md` Act 4 for full details on how this plays out. The sanctum is roughly 40 feet in diameter with the pool in the center. Maren has the high ground on her rock shelf but will descend to talk or fight.

**Tactical notes for combat in the sanctum**:
- The pool is 10 feet in diameter, 5 feet deep. Difficult terrain. Maren can use it freely (swim speed).
- The rock shelf gives Maren half cover (+2 AC, +2 DEX saves) until she descends.
- The sea lavender is flammable — fire spells risk destroying the garden, which enrages Maren.
- The bioluminescence provides dim light throughout. No darkvision needed, but shadows are plentiful.
- The tunnel entrance is the only way in or out (for the player). Maren knows a submerged passage that connects to the sea — her escape route.
