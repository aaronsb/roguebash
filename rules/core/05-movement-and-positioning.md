# Movement and Positioning

---

## Speed

Every creature has a **speed**, which is the distance in feet that the creature can walk in 1 round. This number assumes short bursts of energetic movement in the midst of a life-threatening situation.

- Speed can be modified by race, class features, spells, armor, encumbrance, and conditions.
- A creature with a speed of 0 cannot move.

---

## Movement in Combat

- On your turn, you can move a distance up to your speed.
- Movement can be **broken up** around your action and bonus action. You need not move all at once.
- You can move before, between, and after attacks if you have multiple attacks.
- Each foot of movement spent reduces your remaining movement by 1 foot.

---

## Difficult Terrain

Rubble, undergrowth, steep stairs, snow, shallow bogs, and other obstacles constitute difficult terrain.

- Every **1 foot of movement** in difficult terrain costs **2 feet of speed**.
- The space of another creature (whether hostile or not) counts as difficult terrain.
- Furniture, debris, small trees, and similar features can make a space difficult terrain at the DM's discretion.
- Multiple movement cost penalties (e.g., difficult terrain + crawling) **stack multiplicatively**. Moving 1 foot while crawling through difficult terrain costs 3 feet of movement (1 base + 1 for crawling + 1 for difficult terrain, but calculated as: each extra cost adds 1 foot).

---

## Climbing, Swimming, and Crawling

Each foot of movement spent **climbing, swimming, or crawling** costs **1 extra foot** (2 feet total per foot of movement), or **2 extra feet** in difficult terrain (3 feet total).

**Exceptions:**
- A creature with a **climbing speed** uses that speed to climb without the extra cost.
- A creature with a **swimming speed** uses that speed to swim without the extra cost.

At the DM's option, climbing a slippery vertical surface or one with few handholds requires a successful **Strength (Athletics) check**. Similarly, gaining any distance in rough water might require a successful Strength (Athletics) check.

---

## Prone Movement

- A prone creature can only move by **crawling** (1 extra foot per foot of movement).
- **Standing up** costs movement equal to **half your total speed**. If your speed is 30, standing up costs 15 feet of movement.
- You cannot stand up if you don't have enough movement remaining, or if your speed is 0.
- **Dropping prone** costs no movement.

---

## Jumping

### Long Jump
- **Running long jump** (with at least a **10-foot running start**): Cover a number of feet equal to your **Strength score**. Example: STR 15 = up to 15 feet.
- **Standing long jump** (no running start): Cover a number of feet equal to **half your Strength score**.
- Either way, each foot you cover costs a foot of movement.
- In midair, you can make an obstacle no taller than a quarter of the jump's distance. A DC 10 Strength (Athletics) check may be required to clear a low obstacle during the jump.
- You land prone if you try to land in difficult terrain, unless you succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check.

### High Jump
- **Running high jump** (with at least a **10-foot running start**): Jump a number of feet equal to **3 + your Strength modifier** (minimum 0 feet).
- **Standing high jump** (no running start): Jump a number of feet equal to **half that amount** (3 + STR mod) / 2, rounded down.
- Each foot you cover costs a foot of movement.
- You can extend your arms half your height above yourself during the jump. So you can reach a distance equal to the **height of the jump plus 1.5 times your height**.
- A DC 10 Strength (Athletics) check may allow you to jump higher than normal in certain circumstances.

---

## Squeezing Through Tight Spaces

A creature can squeeze through a space that is large enough for a creature **one size smaller** than it.

While squeezing:
- Every **1 foot of movement costs 2 feet of speed** (like difficult terrain).
- The creature has **disadvantage on attack rolls and Dexterity saving throws**.
- Attack rolls **against** the creature have **advantage**.

---

## Creature Sizes and Space

A creature's **space** is the area in feet that it effectively controls in combat, not its physical dimensions.

| Size | Space | Examples |
|------|-------|----------|
| Tiny | 2.5 ft x 2.5 ft | Imp, sprite, rat, raven |
| Small | 5 ft x 5 ft | Goblin, halfling, gnome, kobold |
| Medium | 5 ft x 5 ft | Human, elf, dwarf, orc |
| Large | 10 ft x 10 ft | Hippogriff, ogre, warhorse |
| Huge | 15 ft x 15 ft | Treant, fire giant, young dragon |
| Gargantuan | 20 ft x 20 ft or more | Ancient dragon, kraken, purple worm |

### Moving Through Other Creatures' Spaces
- You **can** move through a nonhostile creature's space.
- You **cannot** move through a hostile creature's space, unless the hostile creature is at least **two sizes larger or smaller** than you.
- Another creature's space is **difficult terrain** for you (whether hostile or nonhostile).
- You **cannot willingly end your movement** in another creature's space, regardless of size.

---

## Special Movement Modes

### Flying
A creature with a flying speed can use that speed to fly. If a flying creature is knocked **prone**, has its speed reduced to **0**, or is otherwise **deprived of the ability to move**, the creature **falls** unless it can hover or is held aloft by magic (e.g., the *fly* spell).

### Burrowing
A creature with a burrowing speed can use that speed to move through sand, earth, mud, or ice. It cannot burrow through solid rock unless it has a special trait that allows it to do so.

### Hover
A creature that can hover does not fall when knocked prone, when its speed is reduced to 0, or when it is otherwise deprived of movement while airborne.

---

## Falling

A creature that falls takes **1d6 bludgeoning damage per 10 feet fallen**, to a maximum of **20d6** (200 feet).

- The creature lands **prone** unless it avoids taking damage from the fall.
- Falling is instantaneous. If a creature falls during combat, it falls the entire distance immediately.
