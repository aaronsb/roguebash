# Skills

Skills represent specific areas of expertise. Each skill is associated with one ability score, and proficiency in a skill lets a character add their proficiency bonus to checks using that skill.

---

## Skill Check Formula

**d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus** (if proficient in the skill)

If a character has **expertise** in a skill, they add **double** their proficiency bonus instead.

## All 18 Skills by Ability

### Strength
| Skill | Typical Uses |
|-------|-------------|
| **Athletics** | Climbing, jumping, swimming, grappling, resisting being shoved or grappled, any feat of raw physical power |

### Dexterity
| Skill | Typical Uses |
|-------|-------------|
| **Acrobatics** | Maintaining balance on treacherous surfaces, acrobatic stunts (dives, rolls, flips), escaping grapples using agility |
| **Sleight of Hand** | Pickpocketing, planting objects on others, concealing objects on your person, legerdemain, disabling traps with thieves' tools |
| **Stealth** | Moving silently, hiding from enemies, sneaking past guards, avoiding detection. Contested by Perception |

### Constitution
No skills are associated with Constitution. Constitution checks may be called for directly (e.g., holding breath, forced marches, going without food/water).

### Intelligence
| Skill | Typical Uses |
|-------|-------------|
| **Arcana** | Recalling lore about spells, magic items, eldritch symbols, magical traditions, planes of existence, magical creatures |
| **History** | Recalling lore about historical events, legendary people, ancient kingdoms, past disputes, recent wars, lost civilizations |
| **Investigation** | Searching for clues, making deductions, piecing together information, finding hidden objects through logic and reasoning. Distinct from Perception (Investigation is active reasoning; Perception is noticing) |
| **Nature** | Recalling lore about terrain, plants, animals, weather, natural cycles. Knowledge of the natural world |
| **Religion** | Recalling lore about deities, rites, prayers, religious hierarchies, holy symbols, cults, undead |

### Wisdom
| Skill | Typical Uses |
|-------|-------------|
| **Animal Handling** | Calming a domesticated animal, keeping a mount from panicking, intuiting an animal's intentions, controlling a mount during a risky maneuver |
| **Insight** | Determining the true intentions of a creature, detecting lies, predicting someone's next move, reading body language and speech patterns |
| **Medicine** | Stabilizing a dying companion (DC 10), diagnosing illness, determining cause of death, basic field treatment |
| **Perception** | Spotting, hearing, or otherwise detecting the presence of something. Noticing hidden creatures, eavesdropping, detecting ambushes. The most commonly used skill |
| **Survival** | Following tracks, hunting wild game, guiding a party through wilderness, identifying signs of nearby creatures, predicting weather, avoiding natural hazards |

### Charisma
| Skill | Typical Uses |
|-------|-------------|
| **Deception** | Lying convincingly, disguising yourself, creating false impressions, fast-talking, misleading others, hiding the truth through ambiguity |
| **Intimidation** | Influencing through threats, hostile actions, physical violence, or menacing presence. Coercing information or compliance through fear |
| **Performance** | Entertaining an audience through music, dance, acting, storytelling, or other performance arts |
| **Persuasion** | Influencing someone through tact, social graces, good nature, or earnest appeal. Negotiating, making requests, etiquette-based interactions |

## Passive Checks

A passive check is used when the DM wants to secretly determine whether a character succeeds at something without rolling (commonly Passive Perception to detect hidden creatures or traps).

**Formula:** 10 + all modifiers that normally apply to the check

- If the character has **advantage** on the check: +5
- If the character has **disadvantage** on the check: -5

**Example:** A character with Wisdom 15 (+2) and proficiency in Perception (+2 at level 1) has a Passive Perception of **14** (10 + 2 + 2).

---

## Contests

When one creature's effort is directly opposed to another's, the DM calls for a contest. Both participants make ability checks. The higher result wins.

- **Tie:** The situation remains as it was. The initiator fails to change the status quo.
- Example contests: Stealth vs. Perception, Deception vs. Insight, Athletics vs. Athletics (grapple)

---

## Group Checks

When a number of individuals are trying to accomplish something as a group (e.g., the whole party sneaking past guards), the DM may call for a group check.

- Everyone in the group makes the ability check.
- If **at least half** the group succeeds, the whole group succeeds.
- Otherwise, the group fails.

---

## Variant: Skills with Different Abilities

The DM may ask for a skill check using a different ability than its default. For example:
- **Strength (Intimidation):** Flexing muscles to intimidate instead of using Charisma
- **Constitution (Athletics):** A long-distance swim requiring endurance
- **Intelligence (Performance):** Recalling the steps of a complex ritual dance

The character uses the specified ability's modifier but applies their proficiency bonus only if they are proficient in the named skill.

---

## Expertise

Certain class features (Bard's Jack of All Trades, Rogue's Expertise) or feats modify how proficiency works:

- **Expertise:** Double the proficiency bonus for the chosen skill(s). A character with expertise and a +3 proficiency bonus adds +6 instead.
- **Jack of All Trades (Bard):** Add half your proficiency bonus (rounded down) to any ability check that doesn't already include your proficiency bonus.
- **Reliable Talent (Rogue 11th level):** Treat any d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10 for skill checks where you add your proficiency bonus.
