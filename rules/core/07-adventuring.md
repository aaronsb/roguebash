# Adventuring

Rules for travel, survival, environmental hazards, and exploration outside of combat.

---

## Travel Pace

While traveling, a group of adventurers can move at a normal, fast, or slow pace.

| Pace | Per Minute | Per Hour | Per Day (8 hrs) | Effect |
|------|-----------|---------|-----------------|--------|
| Fast | 400 feet | 4 miles | 30 miles | -5 penalty to passive Wisdom (Perception) scores |
| Normal | 300 feet | 3 miles | 24 miles | — |
| Slow | 200 feet | 2 miles | 18 miles | Able to use Stealth |

- The per-day distances assume **8 hours of travel per day**. Characters can push beyond that, but risk exhaustion (see Forced March).
- **Difficult terrain** halves travel distance (e.g., 12 miles per day at normal pace through swamp or dense forest).
- **Mounted travel:** A mount can gallop for about 1 hour, covering twice the normal fast pace distance during that time. Characters with fresh mounts every 8-10 miles can cover greater distances, but this is rare outside densely populated areas.

---

## Forced March

After **8 hours of travel** in a day, characters can push on beyond that limit at the risk of exhaustion.

- For each additional hour of travel beyond 8 hours, each character must make a **Constitution saving throw** at the end of the hour.
- **DC:** 10 + 1 for each hour past 8 hours (DC 10 after 9 hours, DC 11 after 10 hours, DC 12 after 11 hours, etc.).
- **Failure:** The character suffers **1 level of exhaustion**.

## Food and Water

### Food
- A character needs **1 pound of food per day**.
- A character can go without food for a number of days equal to **3 + their Constitution modifier** (minimum 1 day).
- At the end of each day beyond that limit, the character automatically suffers **1 level of exhaustion**.
- A normal day of eating resets the count of days without food to zero.
- Eating **half rations** counts as half a day without food.

### Water
- A character needs **1 gallon of water per day** (or **2 gallons per day in hot weather**).
- A character who drinks only **half that amount** must succeed on a **DC 15 Constitution saving throw** or suffer **1 level of exhaustion** at the end of the day. If the character already has one or more levels of exhaustion, the character takes **2 levels** on a failure.
- A character who drinks **no water** automatically suffers **1 level of exhaustion** at the end of the day. If the character already has one or more levels of exhaustion, the character takes **2 levels**.

### Foraging
While traveling at a slow or normal pace, characters can forage for food and water. The forager makes a **Wisdom (Survival) check**, and the DM determines the DC based on the availability of food and water in the region.

| Food/Water Availability | DC |
|------------------------|-----|
| Abundant (lush forest, river) | 10 |
| Limited (sparse hills, desert oasis) | 15 |
| Very little (barren wasteland, arctic) | 20 |

On a success, the character finds **1d6 + Wisdom modifier pounds of food** and **1d6 + Wisdom modifier gallons of water** (or half as much with a result that barely meets the DC, at DM discretion).

## Light and Vision

### Light Levels
- **Bright light:** Most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources within a specific radius.
- **Dim light (lightly obscured):** Creates a lightly obscured area. Creatures have **disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight**. Dim light includes boundaries of torches/light sources, twilight, a bright full moon.
- **Darkness (heavily obscured):** Creates a heavily obscured area. Creatures effectively suffer from the **blinded** condition (auto-fail sight-based checks, attack rolls against you have advantage, your attack rolls have disadvantage). Outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within unlit dungeons, areas of magical darkness.

### Common Light Sources

| Source | Bright Light | Dim Light | Duration |
|--------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Candle | 5 ft radius | +5 ft (to 10 ft) | 1 hour |
| Lamp | 15 ft radius | +30 ft (to 45 ft) | 6 hours (1 pint of oil) |
| Bullseye Lantern | 60 ft cone | +60 ft (to 120 ft) | 6 hours (1 pint of oil) |
| Hooded Lantern | 30 ft radius | +30 ft (to 60 ft) | 6 hours (1 pint of oil) |
| Torch | 20 ft radius | +20 ft (to 40 ft) | 1 hour |
| *Light* cantrip | 20 ft radius | +20 ft (to 40 ft) | 1 hour (concentration) |
| *Dancing Lights* | 10 ft each (up to 4) | +10 ft each | 1 minute (concentration) |
| *Continual Flame* | 20 ft radius | +20 ft (to 40 ft) | Permanent |
| *Daylight* | 60 ft radius | +60 ft (to 120 ft) | 10 minutes (concentration) |

### Vision Types

#### Darkvision
Many creatures (dwarves, elves, half-elves, gnomes, half-orcs, tieflings, and most monsters) have darkvision.
- Can see in **dim light** within a specified range as if it were **bright light**.
- Can see in **darkness** within that range as if it were **dim light**.
- Cannot discern color in darkness — only **shades of gray**.
- Most racial darkvision has a range of **60 feet**. Some creatures have 120 feet or more.

#### Blindsight
A creature with blindsight can perceive its surroundings **without relying on sight**, within a specific radius. Creatures without eyes (such as oozes) and creatures with echolocation or heightened senses (such as bats and true dragons) have this sense.

#### Tremorsense
A creature with tremorsense can detect and pinpoint the origin of vibrations within a specific radius, provided both the creature and the source of the vibrations are in contact with the same ground or substance. Cannot be used to detect flying or incorporeal creatures. (Not in SRD but commonly used.)

#### Truesight
A creature with truesight can, out to a specific range:
- See in **normal and magical darkness**.
- See **invisible** creatures and objects.
- Automatically detect **visual illusions** and succeed on saving throws against them.
- Perceive the **original form of a shapechanger** or a creature transformed by magic.
- See into the **Ethereal Plane**.

## Falling

A creature that falls takes **1d6 bludgeoning damage per 10 feet fallen**, to a maximum of **20d6** (200 feet of falling).

- The creature lands **prone** if it takes any damage from the fall.
- A fall is instantaneous — if a creature falls during combat, it falls the entire distance immediately.

---

## Suffocation

A creature can hold its breath for a number of minutes equal to **1 + its Constitution modifier** (minimum of 30 seconds).

When a creature runs out of breath or is choking:
- It can survive for a number of rounds equal to its **Constitution modifier** (minimum 1 round).
- At the start of each of its turns after that, it drops to **0 hit points** and is **dying**.
- It cannot regain hit points or be stabilized until it can breathe again.

## Inspiration

The DM can award **inspiration** to a player character, typically as a reward for good roleplaying, creative thinking, or acting in character (especially when it makes things harder for the character).

### How Inspiration Works
- A character either **has inspiration or doesn't** — you can't stockpile multiple uses.
- If you have inspiration, you can spend it when you make an **attack roll, saving throw, or ability check** to gain **advantage** on that roll.
- You must decide to use inspiration **before** the DM says whether the roll succeeds or fails.
- You can give your inspiration to another player character if you think they deserve it.
- A character who already has inspiration cannot gain another instance of it.

## Environmental Hazards

### Extreme Cold
Whenever the temperature is at or below 0 degrees Fahrenheit, a creature exposed to the cold must succeed on a **DC 10 Constitution saving throw** at the end of each hour or gain **1 level of exhaustion**. Creatures with resistance or immunity to cold damage automatically succeed, as do creatures wearing cold-weather gear and creatures naturally adapted to cold climates.

### Extreme Heat
Whenever the temperature is at or above 100 degrees Fahrenheit, a creature exposed to the heat and not carrying water must succeed on a **Constitution saving throw** at the end of each hour or gain **1 level of exhaustion**. The DC starts at **5** and increases by **1** for each subsequent hour. Creatures wearing medium or heavy armor or heavy clothing have disadvantage on the save. Creatures with resistance or immunity to fire damage automatically succeed.

### Strong Wind
A strong wind imposes **disadvantage on ranged weapon attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing**. A strong wind also extinguishes open flames, disperses fog, and makes flying by nonmagical means nearly impossible. A flying creature in a strong wind must land at the end of its turn or fall.

### Heavy Precipitation
Heavy rain or heavy snowfall creates **lightly obscured** conditions, and areas of heavy rain or snow are considered **difficult terrain**. Heavy rain extinguishes open flames.

### Frigid Water
A creature that enters frigid water must succeed on a **DC 10 Constitution saving throw** or gain **1 level of exhaustion**. Creatures with resistance or immunity to cold damage automatically succeed. A creature can be immersed in frigid water for a number of minutes equal to its **Constitution score** before suffering any ill effects. Each additional minute requires the creature to succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw or gain 1 level of exhaustion.
