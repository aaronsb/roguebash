# Ability Scores

The six ability scores define a creature's fundamental capabilities. Every task, check, save, and attack derives from one of these scores.

---

## The Six Abilities

### Strength (STR)
Natural athleticism and bodily power. Governs melee attack rolls and damage (for most weapons), carrying capacity, and physical feats like jumping, climbing, and breaking objects.

### Dexterity (DEX)
Physical agility, reflexes, and balance. Governs ranged attack rolls and damage, AC (when wearing light or no armor), initiative, and feats requiring precision or nimbleness.

### Constitution (CON)
Health, stamina, and vital force. Governs hit points, hit dice, concentration checks for maintaining spells, and endurance against poisons, diseases, and environmental hazards. Constitution has no associated skills.

### Intelligence (INT)
Mental acuity, memory, and reasoning. Governs spellcasting for wizards, and tasks involving logic, education, recall, and deduction.

### Wisdom (WIS)
Perceptiveness, intuition, and willpower. Governs spellcasting for clerics, druids, and rangers, and tasks involving awareness, reading people, and resisting mental influence.

### Charisma (CHA)
Force of personality, eloquence, and presence. Governs spellcasting for bards, paladins, sorcerers, and warlocks, and tasks involving social interaction, persuasion, and deception.

## Generating Ability Scores

### Standard Array
Assign these six values to your abilities in any order:
**15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8**

### Point Buy
You have **27 points** to spend. All scores start at 8. Minimum score is 8, maximum is 15 (before racial modifiers).

| Score | Point Cost |
|-------|-----------|
| 8     | 0         |
| 9     | 1         |
| 10    | 2         |
| 11    | 3         |
| 12    | 4         |
| 13    | 5         |
| 14    | 7         |
| 15    | 9         |

### Rolling
Roll **4d6**, drop the lowest die, and sum the remaining three. Repeat six times. Assign the six totals to your abilities in any order.

---

## Ability Score Limits

- **Player characters:** Maximum ability score of **20**, except where class features, magic items, or other effects explicitly state otherwise (e.g., a Barbarian's Primal Champion feature raises STR and CON caps to 24).
- **Monsters and NPCs:** May have scores as high as 30.

---

## Ability Checks

When a character attempts a task with an uncertain outcome, the DM calls for an ability check.

**Formula:** d20 + ability modifier (+ proficiency bonus if a relevant skill applies and the character is proficient)

### Difficulty Class (DC) Guidelines

| Task Difficulty | DC  |
|-----------------|-----|
| Very easy       | 5   |
| Easy            | 10  |
| Medium          | 15  |
| Hard            | 20  |
| Very hard       | 25  |
| Nearly impossible | 30 |

### Advantage and Disadvantage
- **Advantage:** Roll 2d20, use the higher result.
- **Disadvantage:** Roll 2d20, use the lower result.
- Multiple sources of advantage/disadvantage do not stack. If you have both advantage and disadvantage (regardless of how many of each), they cancel out and you roll normally.

---

## Saving Throws

A saving throw represents an attempt to resist a spell, trap, poison, disease, or similar threat.

**Formula:** d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient in that saving throw)

Each class grants proficiency in two saving throws at 1st level. Unlike skills, saving throw proficiencies are not typically gained from other sources.
