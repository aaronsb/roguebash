# Resting

Adventurers need rest to recover hit points, restore abilities, and avoid exhaustion. There are two types of rest: short rest and long rest.

---

## Short Rest

A short rest is a period of downtime, **at least 1 hour long**, during which a character does nothing more strenuous than eating, drinking, reading, and tending to wounds.

### Hit Dice Recovery During a Short Rest
- A character can spend one or more **Hit Dice** at the end of a short rest.
- For each Hit Die spent, the player rolls the die and adds the character's **Constitution modifier** to it. The character regains hit points equal to the total (minimum of 0).
- The character can decide to spend additional Hit Dice after each roll.
- Hit Dice spent during a short rest are unavailable until recovered (see Long Rest).

### Features That Recharge on a Short Rest
Many class features recharge after a short rest (which also means they recharge on a long rest, since a long rest includes the benefit of a short rest). Notable examples include:
- Warlock spell slots
- Fighter's Action Surge and Second Wind
- Monk's Ki points
- Bard's Bardic Inspiration (at 5th level with Font of Inspiration)
- Channel Divinity (Cleric/Paladin)
- Druid's Wild Shape uses

---

## Long Rest

A long rest is an extended period of downtime, **at least 8 hours long**, during which a character sleeps or performs light activity.

### Requirements
- The rest must be at least **8 hours long**.
- During the rest, a character can **sleep, read, talk, eat, or stand watch** for no more than **2 hours** of the rest period.
- If the rest is interrupted by **1 hour or more** of strenuous activity (walking, fighting, casting spells, or similar), the characters must begin the rest again to gain any benefit.
- A character must have **at least 1 hit point** at the start of a long rest to gain its benefits.
- A character **can't benefit from more than one long rest in a 24-hour period**.

### Benefits of a Long Rest
1. **Regain all lost hit points.**
2. **Recover spent Hit Dice** equal to up to **half the character's total number of Hit Dice** (minimum of 1 die). Example: A 7th-level character has 7 total Hit Dice and can recover up to 3 spent Hit Dice at the end of a long rest.
3. **Reset all features** that recharge "after a long rest" (spell slots, class features, racial abilities, etc.).
4. **Reduce exhaustion** by **1 level**, provided the character has had food and water.

### Features That Recharge Only on a Long Rest
Most class resources recharge on a long rest. Notable examples include:
- Spell slots (for all casters except Warlock)
- Barbarian's Rage uses
- Sorcerer's Sorcery Points
- Paladin's Lay on Hands pool
- Wizard's Arcane Recovery (itself usable once per long rest, but restores slots on a short rest)

---

## Interrupting a Rest

### Short Rest Interruption
If a short rest is interrupted by combat or other strenuous activity, the characters need to start the short rest over again to gain any benefit.

### Long Rest Interruption
If a long rest is interrupted by a period of strenuous activity — at least **1 hour of walking, fighting, casting spells, or similar adventuring activity** — the characters must begin the rest again to gain any benefit from it. Brief interruptions (a single combat encounter of a few rounds, for instance) do not invalidate the rest as long as total strenuous activity during the rest remains under 1 hour.
