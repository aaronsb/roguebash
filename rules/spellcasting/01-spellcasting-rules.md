# Spellcasting Rules

## Spell Levels

- **Cantrips (Level 0)**: Minor spells cast at will without expending spell slots. A cantrip's power often scales with character level.
- **Spell Levels 1st through 9th**: Each spell has a level from 1st to 9th indicating the spell's overall power. Spell level is not the same as character level.

## Spell Slots

Spell slots represent a caster's capacity to cast spells. When a character casts a spell of 1st level or higher, they expend a slot of that spell's level or higher.

- **Regaining Slots**: All expended spell slots are regained after a long rest. Warlocks regain their Pact Magic slots on a short or long rest.
- **Casting at Higher Level**: Some spells have enhanced effects when cast using a higher-level spell slot. The spell description specifies what changes at higher levels.

## Known and Prepared Spells

### Spells Known (Bard, Ranger, Sorcerer, Warlock)
- Characters know a fixed number of spells determined by their class table.
- When gaining a level, they may swap one known spell for another of a level they can cast.
- Known spells are always "prepared" and available to cast.

### Prepared Spells (Cleric, Druid, Paladin, Wizard)
- After each long rest, choose which spells to prepare from the class spell list (or spellbook for Wizards).
- **Number prepared**:
  - Cleric: Wisdom modifier + cleric level
  - Druid: Wisdom modifier + druid level
  - Paladin: Charisma modifier + half paladin level (rounded down), minimum 1
  - Wizard: Intelligence modifier + wizard level
- Minimum of one spell must be prepared.
- Domain/Circle/Oath spells (Cleric/Druid/Paladin) are always prepared and don't count against the limit.

### Spellbook (Wizard)
- Wizards record spells in a spellbook. At 1st level, the spellbook contains six 1st-level wizard spells.
- Each wizard level: add two wizard spells of a level you can cast (free).
- Can also copy spells found on scrolls or in other spellbooks: 2 hours and 50 gp per spell level.
- If the spellbook is lost, a new one can be started but previously copied spells must be re-found.
- Wizards prepare spells from their spellbook each day.

### Spell Save DC
**Spell Save DC** = 8 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier

### Spell Attack Modifier
**Spell Attack Modifier** = proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier

## Spell Components

Most spells require one or more components to cast:

### Verbal (V)
- The caster must speak words of power in a firm voice.
- A character who is gagged, in an area of silence, or otherwise unable to speak cannot cast spells with verbal components.

### Somatic (S)
- The caster must have at least one free hand to perform gestures.
- If holding a shield with a holy symbol (Cleric/Paladin), that hand can provide somatic components for spells requiring a material component.

### Material (M)
- The caster must have access to the listed material component or a spellcasting focus.
- **Spellcasting Focus** (arcane focus, holy symbol, druidic focus, or component pouch): can substitute for any material component that does **not** have a listed gold cost and is **not** consumed by the spell.
- If a material component has a **listed gold piece cost**, the caster must have that specific component.
- Material components are only **consumed** by the spell if the description explicitly says so.
- A caster can use the same hand holding a material component or focus to perform somatic components.

## Concentration

Some spells require the caster to maintain concentration (noted in the Duration line as "Concentration, up to X").

### Concentration Rules
1. **One spell at a time**: A caster can only concentrate on one spell at a time. Casting a new concentration spell ends concentration on the previous one.
2. **Constitution save on damage**: When a concentrating caster takes damage, they must make a Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration.
   - **DC** = 10 or half the damage taken, **whichever is higher**
   - If damage comes from multiple sources, make a separate save for each source of damage.
3. **Incapacitation or death**: Concentration ends immediately if the caster is incapacitated or dies.
4. **Environmental disruption**: The DM may call for a Constitution saving throw (DC 10) if the caster is subjected to harsh conditions (wave crashing over them while on a storm-tossed ship, for example).
5. **Voluntary end**: A caster can end concentration at any time (no action required).

## Ritual Casting

Some spells have the **ritual** tag, meaning they can be cast as a ritual.

- **Casting time**: Takes 10 minutes longer than the spell's normal casting time.
- **No slot expended**: Ritual casting does not use a spell slot.
- **Who can ritual cast**:
  - **Bard**: Can ritual cast any bard spell they know that has the ritual tag.
  - **Cleric**: Can ritual cast any cleric spell they have prepared that has the ritual tag.
  - **Druid**: Can ritual cast any druid spell they have prepared that has the ritual tag.
  - **Wizard**: Can ritual cast any spell in their spellbook that has the ritual tag (does not need to have the spell prepared).
  - **Paladin, Ranger, Sorcerer, Warlock**: Cannot ritual cast (unless granted by a feature such as the Warlock's Book of Ancient Secrets invocation).

## Casting Time

### Action
Most spells require one action to cast, using the Cast a Spell action on the caster's turn.

### Bonus Action
Some spells can be cast as a bonus action. **Important restriction**: On a turn when you cast a bonus action spell, the only other spell you can cast that turn is a cantrip with a casting time of 1 action. This applies even if you haven't used your action yet.

### Reaction
Some spells can be cast as a reaction in response to a specific trigger (described in the spell). A reaction spell can be cast on your turn or on another creature's turn.

### Longer Casting Times
Some spells (such as ritual spells or certain powerful spells) take minutes or hours to cast.
- The caster must maintain concentration during the extended casting time.
- If concentration is broken, the spell fails and the spell slot is expended.

## Range

- **Self**: The spell affects only the caster or originates from the caster (self-targeted area spells specify "Self (X-foot radius)" etc.).
- **Touch**: The caster must touch the target. In combat, this requires a melee spell attack (if the spell calls for an attack roll).
- **Distance (in feet)**: The spell can target a creature, object, or point within the listed range.
- Areas of effect originate from a point within the spell's range (unless range is Self).

## Duration

- **Instantaneous**: The spell's magic flashes into being and is done. The effects (including damage or healing) are not magical after the instant and cannot be dispelled.
- **Timed**: The spell lasts for a specific amount of time (rounds, minutes, hours, or days) and then ends.
- **Concentration**: The spell lasts up to the listed duration but requires concentration (see Concentration rules above).
- **Until Dispelled**: The spell lasts indefinitely until ended by *dispel magic* or a similar effect.

## Areas of Effect

Spells that affect an area describe the area's shape:

### Cone
- A cone extends from the point of origin (usually the caster) in a direction chosen by the caster.
- A cone's width at any point equals its distance from the point of origin.
- A cone's area of effect specifies its maximum length.

### Cube
- A cube's point of origin lies anywhere on a face of the cubic effect.
- The size of a cube is expressed as the length of each side.

### Cylinder
- A cylinder's point of origin is the center of a circle of the cylinder's radius.
- The circle must be on the ground or at the height of the effect.
- The energy spreads from the center point outward to the cylinder's radius and upward/downward to the cylinder's height.

### Line
- A line extends from its point of origin in a straight path up to its listed length.
- The line's width is usually 5 feet unless otherwise stated.
- A line covers an area defined by its width and length.

### Sphere
- A sphere's point of origin is the center of the sphere.
- The sphere extends outward from the center to a radius listed in the spell description.
- A creature is affected if it is within the sphere's radius from the center point.

## Targets

- A spell's description specifies what a caster can target: creatures, objects, a point in space, or the caster themselves.
- **Clear path**: To target something, a caster must have a clear path to it. It can't be behind total cover.
- **Self-targeting**: A caster can target themselves if the spell targets "a creature you can see" or similar wording, as long as they meet the criteria.
- **Invalid target**: If a target is invalid (moved out of range, behind total cover), the spell has no effect on that target but the slot is still expended.

## Combining Magical Effects

- **Same spell, multiple instances**: The effects of the same spell cast multiple times don't combine. Only the most potent effect applies (e.g., highest bonus). If the effects are equal in potency, the most recent casting applies.
- **Different spells**: Effects from different spells stack normally, even if their effects overlap. For example, *bless* and *guidance* both apply to the same creature's ability checks and attack rolls.
- **Same name features**: Same-named game features generally don't stack unless a rule says otherwise.

## Multiclassing and Spellcasting

When a character multiclasses into multiple spellcasting classes:
- **Spell slots**: Determined by combined caster levels using the Multiclass Spellcaster table. Full casters contribute their full class level; half casters (Paladin, Ranger) contribute half their class level (rounded down); Eldritch Knights and Arcane Tricksters contribute one-third their class level (rounded down).
- **Spells known/prepared**: Each class's spells known or prepared are determined separately based on levels in that class alone.
- **Pact Magic**: Warlock Pact Magic slots are tracked separately from other spellcasting slots but can be used to cast non-warlock spells and vice versa.
