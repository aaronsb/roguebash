# Traps

## Trap Categories

### Mechanical Traps
Include pits, arrow traps, falling blocks, rooms that flood, whirling blades, and anything else that depends on a mechanism to operate. Detecting and disabling mechanical traps usually requires **thieves' tools** and a **Dexterity check** or **Intelligence (Investigation) check**.

### Magic Traps
Magical effects such as spell traps and other magical device traps. Detecting magic traps usually requires **detect magic** or a successful **Intelligence (Arcana) check**. Disabling them often requires **dispel magic** or a specific countermeasure.

---

## Trap Elements

### Trigger
What sets off the trap: a pressure plate, tripwire, door handle, spoken word, opening a container, or stepping into an area. A trigger can be discovered with a **Wisdom (Perception) check** to notice it, or an **Intelligence (Investigation) check** to deduce it.

### Effect
What happens when the trap triggers: a pit opens, arrows fire, gas fills the room, a blade swings, a spell is cast, or an alarm sounds.

### Countermeasures
How the trap can be avoided, disabled, or circumvented: disarming it with thieves' tools, casting dispel magic, jumping over the pressure plate, wedging the mechanism, or finding and using the bypass.

## Example Traps

### Pit Trap
*Mechanical trap*

A standard **10-foot-deep pit** covered by a trapdoor that opens when a creature steps on it. The trapdoor is designed to look like the surrounding floor.

- **Detection**: DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check to notice the trapdoor, or DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check
- **Trigger**: Stepping on the trapdoor (weight of 20+ pounds)
- **Effect**: The creature falls 10 feet, taking **1d6 bludgeoning damage** and landing prone
- **Countermeasures**: An iron spike or similar object can be wedged in the hinge to prevent the trapdoor from opening. A successful **DC 10 Dexterity check** using thieves' tools disables the hinge. The pit can also be detected and avoided.

**Variants**:
- **Locking pit trap**: A lock mechanism holds the lid shut after it opens. Escaping requires a **DC 20 Strength check** to force open or **DC 15 Dexterity check** with thieves' tools to pick the lock.
- **Spiked pit trap**: The bottom of the pit is lined with iron spikes. A creature falling in takes **1d6 bludgeoning damage** from the fall plus **2d10 piercing damage** from the spikes.
- **Hidden pit with poison**: Spikes are coated with poison. A creature hit by the spikes must make a **DC 13 Constitution saving throw**, taking **4d6 poison damage** on a failure, or half as much on a success.

### Poison Needle Trap
*Mechanical trap*

A poisoned needle is hidden within a treasure chest's lock, a ## door handle, or something else that a creature might touch or open.

- **Detection**: DC 20 Intelligence (Investigation) check to notice the needle. Examining the lock closely reveals the tiny needle.
- **Trigger**: Opening the lock or handling the trapped object without disarming it
- **Effect**: The creature is pricked and takes **1 piercing damage** and **2d10 poison damage**, and must succeed on a **DC 15 Constitution saving throw** or be **poisoned** for 1 hour
- **Countermeasures**: A successful **DC 15 Dexterity check** using thieves' tools disarms the trap, removing the needle. Unsuccessfully attempting to pick the lock triggers the trap.

### Rolling Sphere
*Mechanical trap*

When 20 or more pounds of pressure are placed on this trap's pressure plate, a hidden trapdoor in the ceiling opens, releasing a **10-foot-diameter rolling stone sphere**.

- **Detection**: DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check to spot the pressure plate, DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check to notice the trapdoor in the ceiling
- **Trigger**: Pressure plate
- **Effect**: The sphere rolls toward the triggering creature in a straight line. Each creature in its path must make a **DC 15 Dexterity saving throw**, taking **4d10 bludgeoning damage** on a failure, or half as much on a success. The sphere stops when it hits a wall or similar barrier. It can be stopped by a creature that has a Strength of 20 or higher with a successful **DC 20 Strength check**.
- **Countermeasures**: Wedging the trapdoor shut prevents the sphere from releasing. Blocking the pressure plate with a weight less than 20 pounds doesn't trigger it.

### Collapsing Ceiling
*Mechanical trap*

This trap uses a tripwire to collapse the supports keeping an unstable section of ceiling in place.

- **Detection**: DC 10 Wisdom (Perception) check to spot the tripwire. DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check to deduce the danger
- **Trigger**: Tripwire
- **Effect**: The ceiling collapses in a **10-foot-square area**. Each creature in the area must succeed on a **DC 15 Dexterity saving throw**, taking **4d10 bludgeoning damage** on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. Once triggered, the area is filled with rubble and becomes **difficult terrain**.
- **Countermeasures**: A successful **DC 15 Dexterity check** using thieves' tools disables the tripwire harmlessly. A successful **DC 15 Strength check** can shore up the supports to prevent collapse (each character in the area must make the check).

### Fire-Breathing Statue
*Magic trap*

This trap is activated when an intruder steps on a hidden pressure plate, releasing a gout of flame from a nearby statue. The statue can be crafted to look like anything, including a dragon, wizard, or demon.

- **Detection**: DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check or **detect magic** reveals an aura of evocation magic on the statue
- **Trigger**: Pressure plate
- **Effect**: The statue exhales a **30-foot cone of fire**. Each creature in the area must make a **DC 13 Dexterity saving throw**, taking **4d10 fire damage** on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.
- **Countermeasures**: **Dispel magic** (DC 13) cast on the statue destroys the trap. A successful **DC 15 Dexterity check** using thieves' tools disables the pressure plate.

### Poisoned Dart Wall
*Mechanical trap*

When a creature steps on a hidden pressure plate, poison-tipped darts shoot from spring-loaded or pressurized tubes cleverly embedded in the surrounding walls.

- **Detection**: DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) to spot the tiny holes in the walls, DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) to notice the pressure plate
- **Trigger**: Pressure plate (20+ pounds)
- **Effect**: Each creature in the area is targeted by **1d3 darts**. Each dart makes a ranged attack with a **+8 attack bonus**. On a hit, a dart deals **1d4 piercing damage**, and the target must succeed on a **DC 15 Constitution saving throw** or take **2d10 poison damage** and be **poisoned** for 1 minute.
- **Countermeasures**: Blocking the holes with cloth or wax prevents the darts from firing. A successful **DC 15 Dexterity check** using thieves' tools disarms the pressure plate.

## Complex Traps

Complex traps work like standard traps, except once activated, a complex trap executes a series of actions each round. A complex trap turns the trap into an encounter, requiring multiple ability checks or attacks to overcome.

A complex trap has all the elements of a simple trap plus:
- **Initiative**: The trap acts on a specific initiative count (often 20)
- **Active elements**: What the trap does on each of its turns
- **Dynamic elements**: How the trap changes over time (escalating danger, moving parts)
- **Constant elements**: Effects that persist each round regardless of the trap's initiative
- **Countermeasures**: Multiple possible actions to disable different components
