# Downtime Activities

Between adventures, characters can spend their time on various activities. A character needs at least **1 day** of downtime to pursue an activity. Days do not need to be consecutive.

---

## Crafting

A character can craft nonmagical objects, including adventuring equipment and works of art. The character must be proficient with tools related to the object being crafted (typically artisan's tools). A character might also need access to special materials or locations necessary to create the item (e.g., a forge for metalwork).

### Crafting Rules
- A character can craft items with a total market value of **5 gp per day** of work
- The character must spend **half the item's market price** in raw materials
- If an item costs more than 5 gp, progress is made in 5-gp increments per day
- **Multiple characters** can combine their efforts toward crafting a single item, provided they all have proficiency with the requisite tools and are working together. Each character contributes 5 gp worth of effort per day.
- While crafting, a character can maintain a **modest lifestyle** without having to pay the daily cost (1 gp/day), or a **comfortable lifestyle** at half the normal cost

### Crafting Examples

| Item | Cost | Crafting Time (1 crafter) |
|------|------|--------------------------|
| Arrows (20) | 1 gp | 1 day |
| Chain mail | 75 gp | 15 days |
| Plate armor | 1,500 gp | 300 days |
| Potion of healing | 50 gp | 10 days |
| Spell scroll (1st level) | 25 gp | 5 days |
| Longbow | 50 gp | 10 days |

> **Note**: Potions and scrolls require the crafter to be a spellcaster with the appropriate spell prepared or known. The crafter must also expend any material components required by the spell.

## Practicing a Profession

A character can work between adventures, allowing them to maintain a **modest lifestyle** without having to pay the 1 gp per day. This benefit lasts as long as the character continues to practice the profession.

If the character is a member of an organization that can provide gainful employment (such as a temple or a thieves' guild), the character earns enough to support a **comfortable lifestyle** instead.

If the character has proficiency in the **Performance** skill and puts their performance skill to use during downtime, they earn enough to support a **wealthy lifestyle**.

---

## Recuperating

A character can use downtime between adventures to recover from a debilitating injury, disease, or poison.

After **three days** of downtime spent recuperating, the character can make a **DC 15 Constitution saving throw**. On a successful save, the character can choose one of the following results:

- **End one effect** on the character that prevents it from regaining hit points
- For the next 24 hours, gain **advantage on saving throws** against one disease or poison currently affecting the character

## Researching

Downtime can be spent researching a particular topic: a monster's capabilities, the location of a ruin, the customs of a culture, the meaning of a mysterious rune, or any other piece of lore.

### Research Rules
- The DM determines whether the information is available, how many days it takes, and whether there are any restrictions (such as needing to seek out a specific individual, tome, or location)
- The DM might also require the character to make one or more ability checks, such as an **Intelligence (Investigation)** check, to find clues pointing toward the information
- Research typically costs **1 gp per day** minimum, representing the cost of accessing resources (books, paying informants, etc.)
- Higher costs may apply for more obscure information or access to restricted libraries
- The character must have access to a **library, sage, or other knowledgeable source** to conduct research

---

## Training

A character can spend downtime learning a new language or gaining proficiency with a new set of tools.

### Training Rules
- **Duration**: 250 days of training
- **Cost**: 1 gp per day
- **Total Cost**: 250 gp
- The character must find a willing **instructor** -- the DM determines whether an appropriate instructor is available. The DM might rule the character doesn't need an instructor for some training, or that training takes more or less time.
- Training can be interrupted and resumed; the character keeps track of progress

### What Can Be Learned
- A new **language**
- Proficiency with a new set of **tools**
- Proficiency with a new **gaming set** or **musical instrument**

> **Note**: Training does not allow a character to gain proficiency in armor, weapons, saving throws, or skills (those are typically gained through class features, feats, or multiclassing).

## Lifestyle Expenses Reference

Characters must pay lifestyle expenses between adventures (or practice a profession to cover them).

| Lifestyle | Cost/Day |
|-----------|---------|
| Wretched | — |
| Squalid | 1 sp |
| Poor | 2 sp |
| Modest | 1 gp |
| Comfortable | 2 gp |
| Wealthy | 4 gp |
| Aristocratic | 10 gp minimum |
