### Difficulty Categories

- **Easy**: The characters aren't threatened. Resources might be expended, but the characters should emerge victorious with no casualties. One or two might need healing.
- **Medium**: One or two scary moments for the characters, but they should emerge victorious with no casualties. More resources expended.
- **Hard**: Could go badly for the adventurers. Weaker characters might get taken out, and there's a slim chance of one or more characters dying.
- **Deadly**: A deadly encounter could be lethal for one or more player characters. Survival requires good tactics and quick thinking, and the party risks defeat.

## Encounter Multipliers

When an encounter includes multiple monsters, apply a multiplier to the total XP of all monsters before comparing against party thresholds. This reflects the increased tactical difficulty of fighting groups.

| Number of Monsters | Multiplier |
|-------------------|------------|
| 1 | x1 |
| 2 | x1.5 |
| 3-6 | x2 |
| 7-10 | x2.5 |
| 11-14 | x3 |
| 15+ | x4 |

### Party Size Adjustments

- **Fewer than 3 characters**: Use the next higher multiplier from the table. For solo monsters, use x1.5 instead of x1.
- **6 or more characters**: Use the next lower multiplier from the table. For groups of 15+, use x3 instead of x4.

> **Note**: The multiplier is for difficulty calculation only. The actual XP awarded to the party is the sum of each monster's individual XP value (without the multiplier).

## Step-by-Step Encounter Building

### Step 1: Determine Party Thresholds
For each character in the party, look up their XP threshold for each difficulty category based on their level. Sum all characters' thresholds together for each category.

**Example**: A party of four 3rd-level characters:
- Easy: 75 x 4 = 300 XP
- Medium: 150 x 4 = 600 XP
- Hard: 225 x 4 = 900 XP
- Deadly: 400 x 4 = 1,600 XP

### Step 2: Total Monster XP
Add up the XP values of all monsters in the encounter.

### Step 3: Apply Encounter Multiplier
Count the number of monsters and apply the appropriate multiplier from the table above to the total monster XP.

### Step 4: Compare to Thresholds
Compare the adjusted XP total to the party's thresholds:
- Below Easy threshold = trivial encounter
- Between Easy and Medium = easy encounter
- Between Medium and Hard = medium encounter
- Between Hard and Deadly = hard encounter
- At or above Deadly threshold = deadly encounter

**Example**: Three ghouls (200 XP each) = 600 XP total. With 3 monsters, apply x2 multiplier = 1,200 adjusted XP. For the party above, this falls between Hard (900) and Deadly (1,600), making it a hard encounter.

### Short Rest Pacing

A typical adventuring day includes **6 to 8 medium or hard encounters** with **two short rests** spaced between them. This pacing ensures classes with short-rest recovery (fighters, warlocks, monks) can use their abilities multiple times while long-rest classes (wizards, sorcerers) must manage their resources.
