## Awarding Experience Points

### Combat XP
Divide the total XP value of all defeated monsters equally among the characters who participated in the encounter. The encounter multiplier is used only for determining difficulty -- award the base XP values, not the adjusted total.

### Noncombat XP
Award XP for overcoming challenges without combat (traps, puzzles, social encounters, exploration discoveries) at the same rate as a combat encounter of equivalent difficulty.

---

## Milestone Leveling

An alternative to tracking XP. The DM awards levels when the party accomplishes significant story milestones.

### When to Award a Level
- Completing a major quest arc or adventure chapter
- Defeating a significant villain or overcoming a major obstacle
- Discovering a critical secret or location
- Accomplishing a goal that advances the campaign narrative

### Guidelines
- Levels 1-3 should pass quickly (1-2 sessions each) to get past the fragile early levels
- Levels 4-10 are the core of most campaigns, with leveling every 2-3 sessions
- Levels 11-16 should slow progression (every 3-4 sessions)
- Levels 17-20 are the climax; level advancement tied to major story beats

### Advantages of Milestone Leveling
- No bookkeeping for XP
- Players don't feel pressured to seek combat for XP
- DM has direct control over progression pacing
- Avoids XP imbalances from missed sessions

### Disadvantages
- Players may feel less agency over their advancement
- No granular reward for individual encounters
- DM must judge pacing carefully to avoid too-fast or too-slow progression
