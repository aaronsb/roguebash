# Monster Rules

> SRD 5.1 Reference — READ ONLY

## How to Read a Stat Block

A monster's stat block contains all the information needed to run the creature. The elements appear in the following order:

### Name, Size, Type, and Alignment
The first line gives the monster's name. The next line gives its size, creature type (and any tags), and alignment.

### Armor Class (AC)
A monster's AC indicates how well it avoids being wounded in combat. The entry includes the source of its AC (natural armor, worn armor, shield, etc.).

### Hit Points (HP)
Hit points are presented as an average followed by the dice expression in parentheses. For example, "52 (8d8+16)" means the monster has 52 hit points on average, calculated from rolling 8d8 and adding 16.

### Speed
A monster's speed tells you how far it can move on its turn. Monsters may have multiple movement modes:
- **Walk** — default land speed
- **Burrow** — digging through earth/stone
- **Climb** — scaling surfaces
- **Fly** — aerial movement (may note hover)
- **Swim** — aquatic movement

### Ability Scores
Six abilities (STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA) with scores and modifiers.

### Saving Throws
Listed only if the monster has proficiency in one or more saving throws.

### Skills
Listed only if the monster is proficient in one or more skills. Some monsters have expertise (double proficiency).

### Damage Vulnerabilities, Resistances, and Immunities
- **Vulnerable** — takes double damage from the listed type(s)
- **Resistant** — takes half damage from the listed type(s)
- **Immune** — takes no damage from the listed type(s)

### Condition Immunities
Conditions that have no effect on the monster.

### Senses
Special senses and passive Perception score.
- **Blindsight** — perceives surroundings without sight within a specific radius
- **Darkvision** — sees in dim light as bright light, darkness as dim light (shades of gray)
- **Tremorsense** — detects vibrations through the ground within a specific radius
- **Truesight** — sees in normal and magical darkness, invisible creatures, into the Ethereal Plane, sees true form of shapechangers/illusions, within a specific radius

### Languages
Languages the monster can speak and/or understand. "—" means no languages.

### Traits
Special abilities that appear before the Actions section. Examples: Amphibious, Pack Tactics, Spellcasting, Innate Spellcasting.

### Actions
Things the monster can do on its turn. Includes attack actions, special actions, and Multiattack if applicable.

**Attack Notation:** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +X to hit, reach X ft., one target. *Hit:* X (XdY+Z) damage type damage.

### Reactions
Actions triggered by specific circumstances (e.g., Parry, Shield).

### Bonus Actions
Actions that use the monster's bonus action (if any).

## Creature Types

There are 14 creature types in D&D 5e:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Aberration** | Utterly alien beings from beyond the planes. Many have innate magical abilities drawn from the creature's alien mind. Examples: aboleth, beholder, mind flayer. |
| **Beast** | Nonhumanoid creatures that are a natural part of the ecology. Some have magical powers, but most are unintelligent. Examples: bear, wolf, dinosaur. |
| **Celestial** | Creatures native to the Upper Planes. Good by nature, though some have fallen. Examples: angel, couatl, unicorn. |
| **Construct** | Made, not born. Some are programmed, others are imbued with sentience. Examples: golem, animated armor, shield guardian. |
| **Dragon** | Large reptilian creatures of ancient origin and tremendous power. Includes true dragons and related creatures. Examples: chromatic and metallic dragons, wyvern, dragon turtle. |
| **Elemental** | Creatures native to the elemental planes. Some are little more than living elements, others have biological forms. Examples: elementals, genies, mephits. |
| **Fey** | Creatures closely tied to the forces of nature. They dwell in twilight groves and misty forests. Examples: dryad, sprite, satyr. |
| **Fiend** | Wicked creatures native to the Lower Planes. Most serve the archdevils and demon lords. Examples: demons, devils, hell hounds. |
| **Giant** | Tower over humans and their kind. Human-like in shape but immense in size. Examples: hill giant, stone giant, ogre, troll. |
| **Humanoid** | The main peoples of the world, both civilized and savage. Includes humans, dwarves, elves, and other playable races, as well as savage races like goblins and gnolls. |
| **Monstrosity** | Frightening creatures that are not ordinary, not truly natural, and almost never benign. Some are results of magical experimentation, others are products of terrible curses. Examples: minotaur, owlbear, roc. |
| **Ooze** | Gelatinous creatures that rarely have a fixed shape. Mostly subterranean, they scavenge and digest anything. Examples: gelatinous cube, gray ooze, black pudding. |
| **Plant** | Vegetable creatures, not ordinary flora. Most are ambulatory. Examples: treant, shambling mound, shrieker. |
| **Undead** | Once-living creatures brought to undead existence through necromancy or a curse. Examples: zombie, skeleton, vampire, lich. |

## Legendary Creatures

Some monsters are legendary -- they are so powerful and so significant that they stand above ordinary creatures. These monsters may have special options in combat.

### Legendary Resistance (3/Day)
If the creature fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead. Most legendary creatures have 3 uses per day.

### Legendary Actions
A legendary creature can take a certain number of special actions (usually 3) outside of its turn. These actions can be used only at the end of another creature's turn. The creature regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn.

Common legendary action costs:
- **1 action** — Simple attacks, detect, or move
- **2 actions** — More powerful abilities
- **3 actions** — The most devastating abilities (e.g., wing attack for dragons)

### Lair Actions
On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), a legendary creature in its lair can use a lair action to cause one of several magical effects. The creature can't use the same lair action two rounds in a row.

### Regional Effects
The mere presence of a legendary creature can alter the surrounding region. When the creature dies, effects fade over 1d10 days.
